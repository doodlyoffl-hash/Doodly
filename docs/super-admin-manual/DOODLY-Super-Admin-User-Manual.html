DOODLY1 · Platform Overview
01Foundations

Platform overview & architecture

DOODLY is a farm-to-doorstep A2 buffalo-milk business. The platform is one system with several front doors — a public storefront, a customer account area, an admin back-office, and a delivery-executive app — all sharing one database.

The four surfaces

Everything a person can see belongs to one of four surfaces. Which surface you land on is decided by your role when you sign in.

Storefront (public)AnyoneBrowse products, read the brand story, sign up, place the first order./
Customer AccountSigned-in customersManage subscriptions, deliveries, wallet, invoices, bottles, referrals, rewards./account/dashboard
Admin Back-officeSuper Admin, Admin, Manager, AccountantRun commerce, operations, supply, finance, growth, content, HR & system./admin/dashboard
Delivery ExecutiveDelivery executivesSee today's route, mark deliveries, collect bottles & cash./delivery/dashboard

DOODLY2 · Roles & Access
02Foundations

Roles & access model

DOODLY uses role-based access control (RBAC). Your role decides which modules you see and what you can do inside them. The Super Admin sits at the top and can grant, restrict or override access for anyone.

The six roles

RoleSeesTypical responsibilities
Super AdminEverythingFull control. The only role that manages Roles, Permissions, GST, and can reverse sensitive money actions.
AdminMost modulesDay-to-day running of commerce, operations, catalogue and content.
ManagerOperations & teamDeliveries, drivers, routes, procurement and staff supervision.
AccountantFinanceExpenses, wallet, GST, payroll, and financial reports.
Delivery ExecutiveDelivery app onlyTheir assigned route, deliveries, bottle & cash collection, attendance.
CustomerAccount area onlyTheir own orders, subscription, wallet, deliveries and rewards.
✅
Best Practice — least privilege

Give each employee the lowest role that lets them do their job. You can always grant a single extra permission to one person (a per-user override) instead of promoting them to a higher role.

Two layers of access

  1. Role defaults — every role starts with a sensible set of permissions defined centrally in code. This is the safety net.
  2. Per-user overrides — the Super Admin can add or remove one specific permission for one specific person, without changing the whole role. Managed in Admin → System → Roles & Permissions.
⚠️
Warning

A per-user override always wins over the role default. If someone can see something they shouldn't (or can't see something they should), check their overrides first before changing the role.

DOODLYAppendix A · FAQ
AAppendix

Frequently asked questions

A customer can't sign up — the password is rejected. Why?

The password must be at least 8 characters and include an uppercase letter, a lowercase letter, a number and a special character. The sign-up form shows a live checklist that turns green as each rule is met. Existing customers are never affected — only new passwords must meet the rule.

Where does a customer land after logging in?

The Home page — unless they were in the middle of a purchase, in which case they return to checkout to finish paying.

A change I made in Admin isn't on the website. What do I check?

Almost always it is saved — the website reads the same database live. Do a hard refresh. If it's still missing, confirm the change was saved (look for the success toast) and that you edited the production environment.

Who can reverse a payment or a reward?

The Super Admin, and for finance items the Accountant. These actions are always audited.

DOODLYAppendix B · Troubleshooting
BAppendix

Troubleshooting

SymptomLikely causeFix
Payment failed at checkoutCard/UPI declined, or gateway keys/limitsAsk the customer to retry with another method; check Commerce → Payments for the failure reason. Nothing is charged on a failure.
Referral link not workingLink missing the code, or programme pausedConfirm the code in Growth → Referrals; share links as /signup.html?ref=CODE.
Orders not auto-assigningNo available drivers, or capacity fullMark drivers available and check capacity in Operations → Auto Assignment; unassigned orders wait in the pending queue.
AutoPay renewal didn't chargeMandate paused, or bank declinedCheck the mandate status in Commerce → Subscription Billing; failed retries pause (never silently cancel) the subscription.
New pincode still shows "not serviceable"Pincode not added, or typoAdd it in Operations → Serviceable Areas — the storefront accepts it instantly.

Glossary

TermPlain-English meaning
Bottle depositA refundable amount (₹120/bottle) the customer pays so glass bottles come back. Returned to their wallet when the empty is collected.
WalletThe customer's in-app balance — from refunds, cashback, referral rewards — usable at checkout.
ReferralInviting a friend with a personal code; a one-time ₹100 reward is paid once the friend reaches an eligible plan.
SubscriptionA recurring plan (7/30/90-day) that delivers milk on a schedule instead of one-off orders.
AutoPayA bank/UPI mandate that renews a subscription automatically before it expires. Opt-in only.
Auto AssignmentThe engine that shares the day's deliveries fairly across available executives.
SKU"Stock Keeping Unit" — a specific sellable item, e.g. 500 ml A2 milk.
GSTIndia's Goods & Services Tax, applied per product and shown on every invoice.
InvoiceThe official GST bill for a payment, downloadable by the customer and admin.
RBAC"Role-Based Access Control" — the system that decides what each role can see and do.

Serviceable areaA pincode DOODLY currently delivers to.
