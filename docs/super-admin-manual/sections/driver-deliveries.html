Deliveries & Delivery Detail

The delivery executive's working list of today's stops and the per-stop screen where each delivery is marked, bottles are counted, proof is captured and problems are reported — the last mile of every DOODLY order.

ℹ️
Where this lives in the product

DOODLY ships two views of the same work. The Deliveries list and Delivery Detail screens documented here are the executive-facing pages (route keys driver/deliveries and driver/delivery). The functional field app the executive actually taps through on their phone is the Delivery Executive Portal (DOODLY_DELIVERY), which renders the very same stops as interactive cards and writes every action to the live backend. Both are described below so a new employee understands the screen they see and the record it creates.

Note — how the live app confirms a delivery

In the production executive app, tapping Mark delivered opens a confirm sheet with three inputs: an optional proof-of-delivery photo (camera), an empty-bottles collected counter (0 … expected), and an optional delivery-notes box. Submitting posts action:"deliver" to the stop API. This is the real path that writes DELIVERED, so proof and the empties count are captured there.

The card fetches the executive's real route from GET /api/delivery/my-route. That route is scoped hard on the server: the role must be delivery executive (or a super admin testing), and only deliveries assigned to their driver record are returned. It defaults to today and, if today has no assignments, falls back to the most recent day that does, so the app always shows real work.

Live location & availability

While a route is running, the app reports GPS to POST /api/delivery/location every ~30 seconds (updating the driver's last-seen position for the admin route view and the customer's tracking map) and stops once every stop is Delivered. The availability pill toggles the executive's shift via /api/driver/availability — Available ↔ Offline. Only Available executives receive new auto-assignments, and an executive mid-trip cannot go offline until they finish or return to the dairy.

✅
Best Practice

Progress the stage honestly in real time — tap On the way when you leave for the stop and Reached when you arrive. The first "On the way" flip is what tells the customer their milk is coming, and the timestamps feed late-delivery monitoring against the 7:00 AM promise. Skipping straight to "Delivered" hides the trip from the people tracking it.

⚠️
Warning — count the empties before you confirm

The bottle count you enter on the confirm sheet is written to the customer's bottle ledger as RETURNED and can affect their refundable deposit. Get it right before tapping Mark delivered — a delivered stop is completed and re-submitting does not change the recorded count.

Frequently asked questions

Can I see yesterday's or another executive's deliveries here?

You only ever see your own route. Today is the default; if today has nothing assigned, the app shows your most recent assigned day (marked as such) so the screen is never empty. Completed past days live under the separate History screen.

What happens to the empty bottles I collect?

The count you enter when confirming a delivery is written to that customer's bottle ledger as a RETURNED entry, which keeps their bottle balance and refundable deposit accurate. Bottles handed over are recorded as ISSUED.

Do I have to enter the delivery OTP to complete a stop?

The OTP box is a verification aid on the detail screen. In the live app, a stop is completed through the confirm sheet (optional proof photo, empties count, notes), which is what actually posts the delivery to the backend.

What does "Report issue" do?

It raises a delivery problem to operations — you pick a type (customer unavailable, wrong address, damaged bottle, payment issue, product issue, delivery failed), a priority and optional comments. It creates an auditable DeliveryIssue record so ops can follow up; the stop stays open.

⛔
Caution — Delivered is a committed record

Marking a stop Delivered stamps the time, writes the bottle ledger, notifies the customer and audits the action. It cannot be quietly undone from the field app — if you tapped it by mistake, tell ops so a supervisor can correct it in the admin Delivery Management screen.
