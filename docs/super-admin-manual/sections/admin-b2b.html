B2B Order Management

Register commercial customers (hotels, cafés, caterers), create bulk milk & dairy orders, move them through a fixed delivery workflow, record payments, raise invoices, and read per-business and portfolio reports — all in one place.

ℹ️
Where this module lives

Open it from Admin → Commerce → B2B Orders. The whole module is one page with five tabs across the top: Businesses, Register, Create Order, Orders and Reports. Every record you create gets a permanent, sequential ID that is never reused: a business is DOO-B2B-000001, an order is B2B-ORD-2026-000125, and an invoice is DOODLY/B2B/2026/00001.

⚠️
Who may use this module

The B2B module is restricted to Admin and Super Admin only. Managers, Accountants, Delivery Executives and Customers cannot open it. This is enforced on the server (canUseB2B in lib/b2b/guard.ts) — hiding the menu item is only a convenience. One action, Delete a business, is reserved for Super Admin alone.

⚠️
Two layers of validation

The on-screen form only blocks a save when name, mobile or address is empty. The production backend (BusinessSchema in lib/b2b/validation.ts) is stricter — it also rejects a bad mobile number, an invalid 6-digit pincode, a name under 2 characters, and a contact person under 2 characters. Always fill every starred field correctly so the record saves cleanly end-to-end.

💡
Tip — set discount and credit limit at registration

The business discount is applied automatically to every future order, and the credit limit triggers an on-screen warning if an order would push the business over its exposure. Setting both here saves you from repeating them on each order.

ℹ️
Price overrides are traceable

Each product row shows its business price ("Biz ₹…") with a slab or retail badge and the GST %. If you change the unit price away from that base, the row is flagged as overridden and a mandatory Override reason appears. On submit, every override is recorded against the order and the business in B2B Pricing — the business's standing default is never altered. If your role lacks override permission the unit-price box is disabled.

⚠️
Credit-limit warning

If the grand total would push the business over its credit limit, a red "⚠ Exceeds credit limit of ₹…" banner appears in the totals panel. It is a warning, not a hard block — use judgment and, if needed, take a payment or raise the limit before submitting.

✅
Best Practice — advance a status only when it is physically true

Move an order to Out for Delivery only when it is actually loaded, and to Delivered only when the customer has it. The workflow is forward-only past each step, so an accidental jump cannot be quietly walked back — the only escape from a mid-workflow status is Cancelled.

Q. Can I change a business's standing price from the order screen?

No. Editing a unit price on an order overrides it for that one order only, and the change is logged with your reason. To change the standing negotiated price, use Pricing & Commercials → (the B2B Pricing screen).

Q. What is the difference between Disable and Delete?

Disable keeps the business and all its order history but marks it Inactive so it is set aside from new work — any Admin can do it and it is reversible with Enable. Delete is a Super-Admin-only soft delete that hides the business from the list while still retaining its order history.

Q. Do I have to invoice before I can take payment?

No. Payments and invoices are independent — you can record a payment on any not-fully-paid order, and generate the invoice before or after. An order gets at most one invoice number, assigned the first time you click Generate invoice.

Q. Why do some order numbers reset each year?

Order and invoice numbers include the year and run a fresh sequence per year (e.g. B2B-ORD-2026-000001, DOODLY/B2B/2026/00001). Business IDs are a single lifetime sequence (DOO-B2B-000001) that never resets and never reuses a number.
