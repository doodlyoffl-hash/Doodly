Chat Support

Manage every conversation the Doodly Assistant has with customers — read live, resolved and escalated chats, watch satisfaction and volume, take over a chat as a human agent, keep a library of canned replies, and tune the assistant's answers without touching code.

ℹ️
What this module is, in plain words

Every DOODLY storefront and customer page carries a floating chat button — the Doodly Assistant. It answers questions about subscriptions, deliveries, products, referrals, wallet, bulk orders, invoices and payments from a built-in knowledge base, and can hand a customer over to a real person on WhatsApp, phone or callback. Chat Support is the admin side of that assistant: it is where you, as Super Admin, see all those conversations gathered from the DOODLY database, jump into any one, mark it resolved or assign it to yourself, curate the ready-made replies your team uses, and add or override the assistant's answers. It is one workspace split into four tabs — Dashboard, Conversations, Canned Responses and Knowledge Base.

⚠️
Who can open and manage chats

The page itself is gated by the chatSupport permission. If your role cannot view it, the whole workspace is replaced by an "Access restricted" panel ("You don't have permission to view chat support."). On the server the rule is broader than one permission: viewing chats is allowed to anyone with chatSupport:view, support:view, or customers:view; managing chats (assign, resolve, send, close, escalate, delete…) needs chatSupport:edit or support:edit. In practice that means the Super Admin and Admin get full control, the Support role can view and handle chats, and read-only viewers get the list without the action buttons. Every change is written to the shared Audit Log as chat.<action>.

💡
Tip — the page stays live on its own

When you open Chat Support, the page fetches the latest chats from /api/admin/chat and then auto-refreshes the Dashboard every 20 seconds so your counts stay current without a manual reload. The polling is considerate: it pauses while you have a conversation transcript open or a pop-up showing, and it only refreshes while you are on the Dashboard tab, so it never yanks you out of a chat you are reading. If the banner turns red — "⚠ Backend offline… start next-app" — the counts you see are the last good copy held on the page.

ℹ️
Where the conversations come from

Each chat here is a real ChatSession in the DOODLY database, created when a customer talks to the Doodly Assistant on the storefront. A background sync mirrors those sessions (and their messages) into the admin view, translating the server's status names into the three you see on screen — a database Waiting chat shows as Active, and a Closed chat shows as Resolved. On a fresh production site the list is deliberately empty until customers actually start chatting — sample conversations are never seeded on the live domain.

ℹ️
The API tracks more than the six cards show

The dashboard cards above are the headline figures. The underlying /api/admin/chat?view=dashboard endpoint computes several more that power reporting: waiting, AI-handled vs human-handled counts, the number of CSAT responses, and resolved today, plus the top eight topics. These are available to any tooling built on the API even though the on-screen dashboard focuses on the six most useful cards.

ℹ️
These two buttons are database-backed and instant

When you click Mark resolved or Assign to me on a chat that exists in the database, the action is sent straight to the server, a small toast confirms it ("Marked resolved" / "Assigned to you"), and the list re-syncs so the status and handler update everywhere. If the server rejects it because your role can't manage chats, you'll see "Only Support / Admin can manage chats (403)." A rare chat that exists only locally (not yet in the database) is handled on the page instead, so the buttons always do something sensible.

Overrides change what customers see

An override is applied on top of the assistant's answer the moment you save it — the next customer who asks about that topic sees your wording, on the live storefront widget. Keep overrides accurate and on-brand; if in doubt, remove the override to fall back to the tested default.

ℹ️
Everything is audited

Every mutation writes an entry to the shared Audit Log as chat.<action> (for example chat.close, chat.assign). Status changes and assignments also drop a System message into the transcript itself, so the conversation carries its own history of who did what and when.

✅
Best Practice — assign before you reply, resolve when you're done

When you step into a customer chat, click Assign to me first. That flips the chat to Human-handled and stamps your name on it, so a teammate can see at a glance that it's covered and won't double-answer. When the issue is settled, click Mark resolved — it closes the chat, clears any escalation flag, and moves it out of the Active count. Both actions leave a System note in the transcript, giving you a clean, auditable record of ownership and outcome.

✅
Best Practice — fix a wrong answer in seconds with a Knowledge Base override

If the assistant is giving an outdated or off-brand answer (say, an old price or delivery cut-off), don't wait for a code change. Open the Knowledge Base tab, copy the topic id from the table, and paste a corrected Override answer. It goes live to the storefront widget the moment you save. Once the underlying answer is properly updated, Remove the override so you fall back to the tested default.

Frequently asked questions

Where do these conversations come from?

From the Doodly Assistant — the chat button on every storefront and customer page. Each conversation is saved to the DOODLY database and mirrored into this admin view. You are reading real customer chats, not a simulation.

Can I type a reply to the customer from this screen?

The current admin screen focuses on reading a chat and two actions — Mark resolved and Assign to me. The backend already supports agent replies and internal notes (the send and note actions), which flip a chat to Human-handled; those are available to tooling built on the API. For live back-and-forth, the assistant hands customers to WhatsApp, phone or a callback.

What's the difference between "Escalated" and "Assigned"?

Escalated means the chat was flagged for a human (often because the customer asked to talk to someone). Assigned means a specific staff member owns it. You typically assign an escalated chat to yourself, which also switches Handled by to Human.

If I delete a chat, is it gone forever?

No. Deletes are soft — the record is marked deleted (hidden from the list) but preserved and can be restored. This keeps your history and audit trail intact.

Does changing a Knowledge Base override affect what customers see immediately?

Yes. Once you save an override, the assistant uses your wording for that topic straight away on the live widget. Remove the override to return to the built-in answer.

Why does "Avg response time" always say 1.0s?

The assistant answers instantly from its knowledge base, so the dashboard shows a fixed, representative 1.0s as a headline. It is an indicative figure for how fast the AI replies, not a per-conversation stopwatch.
