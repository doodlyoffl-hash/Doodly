B2B Pricing

Give every commercial customer its own negotiated product prices, quantity slabs and effective dates — with a full change history, bulk tools, reports and an order-time override log that never touches the saved default.

ℹ️
What this screen is for

Retail (storefront) customers pay one published price. A hotel, café or reseller usually negotiates a different rate. B2B Pricing is where you record that negotiated rate per business, per product, so every B2B order automatically fills in the right price. If a business has no custom price for a product, the order simply uses the normal retail price. Prices are stored as whole rupees on screen; the DOODLY database keeps them as paise (₹1 = 100 paise) for exactness.

⚠️
Who can do what

Viewing pricing is open to Admin and Super Admin. Every change — editing a price, setting slabs, bulk updates, copy/import, scheduling, deactivating a business's pricing and editing settings — is restricted to the Super Admin. The server (/api/b2b/pricing) additionally blocks any role that is not Admin or Super Admin with a 403 Forbidden. Managers, Accountants, Delivery Executives and Customers cannot open this module.

ℹ️
Note — "Promotional" resets from retail

Increase and Reduce adjust the current business price. Promotional is different: it computes the new price as a percentage off the retail price, ignoring whatever custom price was there. Use Promotional for festival or launch offers, and Reduce for shaving a few percent off an already-negotiated rate.

ℹ️
Note — where overrides come from

Overrides are created over in B2B Orders → Create order. When an authorized user changes a line price while building an order and gives a reason, that change is logged here (recordOverride) and the order is charged the new amount — but the business's saved price is left exactly as it was. Order-time overriding is allowed for the Super Admin, or anyone granted the pricing override permission or B2B edit permission.

⚠️
Warning — no duplicate rules

The server refuses a second active rule for the same business + product + variant + minimum quantity and returns the existing rule's code (for example "A pricing rule already exists … B2BP-000004. Edit it instead."). To change that price, edit the existing rule rather than creating a new one.

Does changing a business price affect orders already placed?

No. A price change only affects new orders from that point. Past orders keep the price they were charged, and any order-time override is logged separately in Order Overrides.

What happens if a business has no custom price for a product?

The order simply uses the normal retail price. You don't have to price every product — only the ones you've negotiated.

Why are there two prices for one product (₹78 and ₹77)?

Those are quantity slabs. Small orders pay the higher rate; larger orders automatically drop to the lower slab. The engine picks the slab that matches the order quantity, as long as slab pricing is enabled in Settings.

Is GST included in the price I type?

The price you type is the pre-GST unit price. The retail column shows the product's GST %, and the database also stores a GST-inclusive figure derived from the GST rate on the rule.

✅
Best Practice — set up a new B2B customer's pricing fast

Register the business first under B2B Orders → Register. Then, if a similar customer already exists, use Copy / Import / Export → Copy pricing to clone their price map, and tweak the few products that differ. Finish by opening Customer preview to confirm the customer sees exactly the rates you intended.
