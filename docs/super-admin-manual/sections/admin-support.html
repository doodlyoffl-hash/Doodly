Support Tickets

A full customer-service ticket desk — every complaint or query becomes a numbered ticket you can prioritise, assign to a teammate, reply to, keep internal notes on, track against an SLA due-date and drive through its whole lifecycle from Open to Closed, all recorded live in the DOODLY database.

ℹ️
One page, three working surfaces

The whole desk lives at one screen (/admin/support.html, route key admin/support, block type supportBoard, mount #supportMount). It is wired by assets/js/layout.js → wireSupportBackend() and talks to one endpoint, /api/admin/support (service: next-app/lib/support/service.ts). On load it paints a KPI strip, a filter toolbar + tickets table, and — when you click Manage on any row or the header's New ticket button — a slide-over ticket drawer / new-ticket form. Each of those is documented below as its own screen.

ℹ️
Where tickets come from

Tickets are created two ways, both landing in the same desk: (1) staff raise them here with New ticket; (2) a signed-in customer raises one from their account (POST /api/account/support), which sets the ticket's customerId, name, email and phone from their authenticated profile — never trusted from the browser. The customer only ever sees a safe subset of their own ticket (subject, category, status, priority, dates) — never the assignee, SLA, internal notes, resolution text or tags.

ℹ️
The green live banner

Under the strip a status line reads, for example, "● Live — 128 ticket(s) · 14 unresolved · 3 overdue from the DOODLY database". Unresolved is every ticket that is not Resolved or Closed; overdue matches the Overdue KPI. If the backend is unreachable you get a red banner ("⚠ Backend offline…"); if your role lacks access you get "⚠ Your role can't view Support (403)."

💡
The cards are read-only and self-refresh

There are no buttons on the KPI strip. It re-computes whenever the page loads and again after any ticket action (assign, reply, status change, close, delete) via refreshSupportKpis(), so the numbers you see always reflect the queue you're looking at.

ℹ️
Bulk & merge exist in the API, not on this board

The endpoint supports bulk status change / delete / restore (action:"bulk", up to 1000 IDs) and duplicate-merge (action:"merge", closes one ticket into another). The desk UI does not surface checkboxes or a merge button yet — these are backend-ready capabilities, not buttons you'll see today.

ℹ️
How the SLA due-date is set

When the ticket is created, DOODLY stamps a first-resolution target (slaDueAt) based on priority: Urgent = 4 hours, High = 8 hours, Medium = 24 hours, Low = 48 hours from now. That date drives the SLA column and the Overdue KPI. Changing priority later does not re-stamp the original due-date.

💡
Ticket numbers

Numbers are sequential and human-friendly — TK-2042, TK-2043, … generated from the current ticket count with a uniqueness check, so they never collide.

⚠️
The three dropdowns act the moment you change them

Status, Priority and Assignee save on change — there is no separate "Save" button for them. Each one fires its own API call and re-renders the drawer with a toast. Be deliberate: picking a value is the action.

✅
Reply vs. note — get this right

Add customer reply is customer-facing and starts/affects the SLA response clock; Add internal note is staff-only and invisible to the customer. Never put an internal comment ("customer is being difficult", pricing you can offer, teammate handover details) in a reply. When in doubt, use a note.

✅
Best practice — triage by SLA and priority every shift

Start each shift on the Overdue and High Priority KPIs, then filter the table to Urgent and High. Assign every unowned ticket so nothing sits in Open, give the customer a first reply quickly (it stamps firstResponseAt and improves your Avg Response), and always close with a short resolution note so the next person can see how it was solved. Use Waiting for Customer honestly — it keeps genuinely-blocked tickets out of your "needs action" view.

Frequently asked questions

Does the customer see my internal notes?

No. Internal notes (amber, badged "internal note") are staff-only. Only customer replies are meant for the customer, and the customer's own account view is further limited to the subject, category, status, priority and dates — never notes, assignee, SLA or resolution text.

Where does "Resolved Today" come from, and does closing count?

It counts tickets whose resolvedAt is on or after midnight today. Because Close ticket stamps both closedAt and resolvedAt, closing a ticket today also counts it as resolved today.

What decides whether a ticket is "Overdue"?

An open ticket (anything not Resolved or Closed) whose slaDueAt is earlier than the current time. The due-date is set at creation from the priority: Urgent 4h, High 8h, Medium 24h, Low 48h.

Can I delete a ticket permanently?

Not from this desk. Delete is a soft delete — it sets deletedAt and hides the ticket, but the record and its history are preserved and can be restored through the API. Every delete is also written to the audit trail.

Who can be an assignee?

Any staff user — the assignee dropdown lists all users whose role is not customer or delivery executive, plus an Unassigned option. Assigning an Open ticket automatically moves it to Assigned.

ℹ️
Everything here is audited

Every mutation — create, assign, priority, status, reply, note, close, reopen, merge, delete, restore, bulk — writes an audit record (support.<action>) with the actor and target. You can review the full trail on Admin → System → Audit Logs.
