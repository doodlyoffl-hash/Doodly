Quality Testing

Test every raw-milk batch for fat, SNF, temperature and adulteration before it is trusted — pass or fail it, approve or reject it, and keep raw-milk inventory perfectly in step with those decisions.

The Quality Testing screen is the lab desk of the Supply team. Each day, farmers' milk arrives as procurement batches; each batch has to be screened before it can become part of the milk DOODLY bottles and sells. This screen is where a quality officer records those lab readings — fat percentage, SNF (Solids-Not-Fat) percentage, lactometer/density and temperature — and where the system decides whether the batch passes or fails against the configurable acceptance rules. It also gives supervisors a batch-approval workflow that adds accepted milk to, or pulls rejected milk out of, the raw-milk inventory count.

ℹ️
Note — one shared record, three screens

A "quality record" is not a separate table. It is a procurement batch (the same Procurement row you see under Admin → Supply → Procurement and in that farmer's history under Admin → Supply → Farmers) with an optional lab test (QualityTest) attached. Recording a test here writes to that shared batch; approving or rejecting it flips the same accepted flag the Procurement desk uses. There is no duplicate data to reconcile.

⚠️
Warning — quality decisions move real inventory

Failing a test, or rejecting a batch, does more than change a label. If the batch had already been counted into raw-milk stock, the system automatically removes those litres from the MILK_RAW inventory item. Approving a batch adds its litres back. Treat every pass/fail and approve/reject as a stock movement, not just a note.

What the Result badge means

The badge is derived from the batch — whether it has a lab test yet, whether that test passed, and whether the batch is accepted. There are four possible states:

BadgeColourMeaningUnderlying condition
PassedGreenA test was recorded and it met the standard.Test exists and passed = true.
FailedRedA test was recorded and it fell short.Test exists and passed = false.
PendingAmberAccepted into the day's collection but not yet tested.No test and accepted = true.
RejectedGreyNot accepted, and never tested.No test and accepted = false.

Grade (A / B / C)

Alongside the pass/fail status, every batch is given a letter grade calculated on the fly from the current acceptance rules (Screen 4). You see this grade inside the Manage drawer.

GradeWhen it is awarded
CFat is below Minimum Fat % or SNF is below Minimum SNF % — i.e. it fails the standard.
AFat is at least Minimum Fat % + 1.5 and SNF is at least Minimum SNF % + 0.5 — comfortably above standard.
BMeets the minimum but not the "A" margin — everything in between.

Tip — read the banner first

The green "● Live" banner tells you the numbers came straight from the DOODLY database and how many records loaded. A red banner means you are looking at stale or empty data — either the backend is offline or your role lacks quality:view. Never make a pass/fail call off a red banner.

ℹ️
Note — approve/reject is idempotent on inventory

The stock movement only fires when the accepted state actually changes. Approving an already-approved batch, or rejecting an already-rejected one, is a safe no-op (inventory delta 0). You cannot double-count or double-subtract by clicking twice.

⚠️
Warning — rules change the grade of the whole queue, live

Because the A/B/C grade and the pass/fail hint are computed from these thresholds each time the list loads, raising the Minimum Fat % or Minimum SNF % can instantly re-grade batches you are about to test — and can turn an "A" into a "B" or a borderline batch into a fail. Change them deliberately, ideally between shifts, not mid-testing.

✅
Best Practice

Set the acceptance rules once with your dairy technologist to match your FSSAI and internal standards, then leave them alone. Record every test through the modal so the Result is decided by the rules, not by opinion — that consistency is what makes the Compliance KPI and the farmer quality history trustworthy. Reserve manual overrides for genuine lab-sheet discrepancies, and always add a reject reason when you fail a batch so the farmer's history explains itself.

ℹ️
Note — bulk approve / reject exists behind the scenes

The backend also exposes POST /api/admin/quality/bulk, which approves or rejects many batches at once (up to 1000 ids), syncing raw-milk inventory per batch and writing one audit entry (quality.bulk.approve / quality.bulk.reject). It also needs quality:edit. The current Quality Testing screen has its toolbar turned off, so there is no on-screen "select many → approve" control; this route is available for scripted or future bulk operations rather than a button you click today.

Frequently asked questions

Can I edit a test after I have saved it?

No — a batch can hold only one QualityTest, and trying to test it again returns a conflict. What you can still change is whether the batch is accepted: open its Manage drawer and use Approve or Reject. That is the lever that controls inventory, and it can be flipped as many times as needed.

What is the difference between "Failed" and "Rejected"?

Failed means a lab test was recorded and the milk did not meet the standard (a test exists, passed = false). Rejected means the batch was simply never accepted and has no test at all. A fail automatically un-accepts the batch, so a failed batch is also excluded from inventory — but the badge shows "Failed" because there is a test behind it.

Why does the Result flip by itself when I type the readings?

The modal reads the live acceptance rules and compares your fat and SNF against the Minimum Fat % and Minimum SNF %. If both meet the minimum it pre-selects Passed; otherwise Failed. It is a guide to keep officers consistent — you can still override the selection before saving.

Does approving a batch here change anything on the Procurement screen?

Yes. Approve/Reject flips the very same accepted flag the Procurement desk sees, and the batch's QC badge updates on both screens. There is one shared record, so the two teams never disagree about a batch's status.

Where do the temperature and density limits actually get enforced?

They are stored on the acceptance rules and shown to the tester, and temperature feeds the "Avg temp" KPI. The automatic pass/fail decision, however, is driven specifically by the fat and SNF minimums; temperature and density are the reference bounds your lab checks against and records, with the final call made by the tester and the Approve/Reject decision.
