Payments

The single, canonical money ledger for DOODLY — every rupee collected through a Razorpay order, a subscription renewal, an Auto-Pay charge, a cash-on-doorstep hand-off or a manually recorded payment lands here, where staff can search it, refund it, reconcile it, send a receipt and export it for accounting.

ℹ️
Two surfaces, one ledger

The Payments item under Admin → Commerce is served by two front-ends that both read the same database tables through the same APIs. (1) The storefront admin page (/admin/payments.html) shows four live KPIs and a read-only ledger table with the shared DataTable toolbar. (2) The full Payments board in the Next.js admin app adds five working tabs — Dashboard, Payments, Record Payment, Reports, Gateways — with refunds, receipts, reconciliation and CSV exports. This manual documents the complete capability set. Money is always stored as integer paise (₹1 = 100 paise) and rendered in ₹; every change appends an immutable PaymentEvent so the history can never be quietly edited.

ℹ️
How records get into the ledger

The ledger is a superset of every payment source. Paid order Payment rows and paid SubscriptionBilling rows are synced in automatically (both a one-time back-fill and forward on every new event), so the ledger is never missing a gateway payment. Staff add MANUAL rows for cash or off-gateway settlements. Each row carries a source of ORDER, SUBSCRIPTION, AUTOPAY, MANUAL or B2B, and a unique code like PAY-000042. Sync is idempotent — re-running it updates the existing row rather than creating a duplicate.

⚠️
Refunds are one-directional

A refund can only be raised against a Captured or Part-refunded payment, and never for more than the remaining refundable balance. A Pending or Failed payment cannot be refunded — there is nothing to return yet. Each refund gets its own RFND- reference and a customer notification fires immediately, so double-check the amount and the "to wallet" toggle before you press Process.

ℹ️
The Payments module is finance-only

Only Super Admin, Admin and Accountant have the payments module in the permission matrix, and all three hold it at "full" — so all three can view, record, edit and refund. Support, operations, procurement, quality, inventory, marketing and delivery roles have no payments access at all and never see this screen. Server-side requirePermission is the real security boundary; the client menu filter is convenience only.

✅
Best Practice — reconcile against the bank, then refund from the ledger

Run Reports for the period, export the CSV and match it to the Razorpay settlement report using the Payment ID and Transaction ID columns. Tick the matched rows and hit Reconcile so the ledger records what's been banked. Only refund from the payment's own detail panel (never by editing the customer's wallet directly) — that way the refund is capped at the refundable balance, gets its own RFND- reference, notifies the customer and, when you choose "to wallet", credits them in one auditable step.

Frequently asked questions

Q. A customer paid by UPI to our staff phone. How do I record it?

Open Record Payment, search and pick the customer, optionally link the order or billing cycle, set method UPI, enter the amount and the UPI reference, tick Mark as paid, and press Record payment. It appears in the ledger as a Captured MANUAL payment.

Q. What's the difference between "Amount" and "Net"?

Amount is the gross charged. Net is what actually came in through the gateway or cash, i.e. gross minus any wallet balance the customer applied. Reports total both so you can see wallet usage separately.

Q. If I refund "to wallet", does the customer get real money back?

No — "to wallet" credits the refund to the customer's DOODLY wallet for their next purchase. Leave it unticked to signal the money is going back to the original payment method; either way the refund is logged with its own reference and the customer is notified.

Q. Why do some payments show a "✓" on the Source column?

That means the payment has been reconciled — matched to a bank settlement — either individually from its detail panel or via the bulk Reconcile action.

Q. Can I edit or delete a payment after it's recorded?

No. The ledger is append-only for integrity — you can refund, add notes, reconcile, resend receipts and log retry attempts, and every one of those actions is written to the payment's timeline. There is no "delete", by design.
