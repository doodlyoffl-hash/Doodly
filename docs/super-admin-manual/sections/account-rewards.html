Rewards (Pure Rewards)

The customer's DOODLY Pure Rewards page — their loyalty tier, points balance, live tier progress, expiring-points warnings, a one-tap "redeem points to wallet" tool, the full list of ways to earn, every tier's benefits, and a complete points history — all driven by the server-authoritative loyalty ledger.

ℹ️
Where this screen lives — and where staff configure it

This is a customer-facing page. A signed-in customer opens it from the account sidebar under Rewards → Rewards (URL /account/rewards.html). Everything on it is display + the redeem action — the points ledger, tiers, earning rates and expiry are all decided on the server. A Super Admin never edits reward rates from here; staff use the separate Admin loyalty module (the #loyaltyAdminMount screen backed by /api/admin/loyalty/config, /members and /adjust) to change earning rates, tiers, redemption ratio, expiry and to grant or deduct a member's points. This manual page documents what the customer sees so support staff can walk a caller through it and read exactly the same numbers.

ℹ️
How the page is assembled (from the route manifest)

The account/rewards route stacks three blocks in order: a heading ("DOODLY Pure Rewards — The more you stay fresh, the more you're rewarded."), the loyaltyProgram block (mount #loyaltyMount, built by loyalty.js), and a puzzleCard block (the Monthly Puzzle Challenge card, mount #puzzleCardMount). This section documents the loyaltyProgram — the main rewards experience — plus the compact rewards card that appears on the account dashboard.

What the page loads

On open, a signed-in customer first sees a skeleton, then the page calls GET /api/account/rewards and renders from the returned rewards summary. The summary is cached for 20 seconds client-side and force-refreshed after any redeem. Guests and demo visitors never see another customer's data — the page self-gates (see states below).

Tier hero & progress

The hero shows the tier badge and name, the big available-points number, and a progress bar filled to nextTier.progressPct. Below it: "lifetimeEarned / nextTier.min points" and "pointsAway points to <next tier>". At the top tier the bar is full and it reads "You've reached <tier> — Top tier unlocked 🎉". The tier icon set is 🥉 Fresh · 🥈 Silver · 🥇 Gold · 💎 Platinum. Tier follows lifetime earned, so spending points never demotes a member.

ℹ️
Bonus campaign banner

When an admin sets a campaignMultiplier above 1 with a future campaignEndsAt, a banner appears: "✨ Bonus campaign live — earn X% extra points until <date>", where X = (multiplier − 1) × 100 (a 1.05 multiplier shows "5% extra"). The multiplier only boosts spend/activity earns that opt in (order points, subscribe and renewal), not fixed one-off bonuses.

Expiring-points notice

Shown only when expiring.within30Days > 0: "⏳ N points expiring within 30 days — next M on <date> (D days). Redeem them for wallet credit below." Points are lots that each expire 12 months after they were earned; the server's expiry cron zeroes past-due lots and sends 30-day and 7-day reminders (in-app + email, and WhatsApp where opted in).

ℹ️
The maths (default config)

The ratio is 10 points = ₹1 and the minimum is 100 points (both admin-tunable). Credit = (points ÷ ratio) × ₹1, so 750 points → ₹75. Points must convert to a whole rupee — the input steps by the ratio and the server rejects any amount that isn't an exact multiple. The credit lands in the customer's DOODLY Wallet (a WalletTxn of kind "loyalty"), so it can then be spent on any order, renewal or top-up.

✅
Best Practice — idempotent, so a retry never double-spends

Every redemption is recorded as a unique ledger row and mirrored to a single wallet credit. If a caller taps "Redeem" twice or their connection drops after the credit posted, the server returns the original credit instead of spending points again. When helping a customer who is unsure whether a redemption went through, check their Wallet history for a "Loyalty redemption" credit before re-attempting — don't advise a second redeem.

FAQ & terms (collapsible)

Two expandable panels sit at the foot of the page. The FAQ answers: how to join (every registered customer is auto-enrolled), how to redeem (ratio + minimum → wallet credit), when points expire (12 months, with 30- and 7-day reminders), what earns points, and whether points can be transferred or cashed out (no — wallet credit only). The Terms & conditions panel lists seven rules, including that points are earned only after an eligible action completes successfully, bottle-return points need bottles in good condition, referral rewards credit after the friend's qualifying subscription payment, points can't be cashed out or transferred, rates may change (never affecting points already earned), and that fraud/duplicate/self-referral accounts can have points reversed and be suspended.

💡
Tip — what the customer gets notified about

Members are messaged (in-app, and email/WhatsApp where they've opted in) when they earn a welcome, profile, subscription, referral, streak, birthday, anniversary or puzzle-win bonus, when they cross a tier threshold, and 30 and 7 days before points expire. Because every earn is idempotent, a webhook retry or page refresh never sends a duplicate.

⚠️
Warning — points are not cash

Points can't be exchanged for cash, sold, or transferred between accounts — they are redeemable only as DOODLY Wallet credit. If an account is permanently deleted, all unused points expire. DOODLY may reverse points and suspend accounts involved in fraud, duplicate accounts, self-referrals or automated abuse. Never promise a customer a cash payout of points.

FAQ

Q. How does a customer join Pure Rewards?

There is nothing to sign up for — every registered DOODLY customer is automatically enrolled and starts at Fresh Member. Signing in is all that's needed to see the page.

Q. Do points expire?

Yes — each batch of points is valid for 12 months from the date it was earned (config expiryDays, default 365). The server reminds the customer 30 and 7 days before any points expire, and a cron job zeroes past-due points. Redeeming always consumes the points closest to expiry first, so nothing is wasted.

Q. Can support add or remove a customer's points?

Yes, but not from this customer page. Staff with loyalty-edit permission use the Admin loyalty module's Adjust tool (POST /api/admin/loyalty/adjust): a positive number grants points, a negative number deducts them (oldest-expiry-first), and a reason is required and recorded for the audit. The change appears in the customer's history as "Adjustment".

Q. Why doesn't the tier drop when a customer redeems points?

Tier is based on lifetime earned, not the current balance, so spending points never demotes a member — only the available-points number goes down.

Q. Where does the redeemed money go?

Into the customer's DOODLY Wallet as a "loyalty" credit. From there it can be spent on any order, renewal or top-up. Money stays in one place — the redemption never creates a separate cash-out.
