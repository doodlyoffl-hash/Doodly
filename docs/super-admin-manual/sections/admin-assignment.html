Auto Assignment

Automatically distribute each morning's confirmed deliveries across your available delivery executives — capped at 45 bottles per trip — queue the overflow, and re-fill executives the moment they return to the dairy.

The Auto Assignment board is the operations control room for the "last mile". Every confirmed delivery for a slot is packed onto executives up to a strict 45-bottle carrying capacity per trip (counted by bottles, not by customers). Anything that does not fit drops into a Pending Assignment Queue; when an executive comes back to the dairy, the next batch is pulled from that queue automatically. The screen adds workload balancing, nearest-neighbour route optimisation, a full executive status workflow, and manual override by drag-and-drop, lock and unassign. A live activity feed records every move.

ℹ️
Note — the board is a planner that overlays a real engine

The interactive board (assign.js / DOODLY_ASSIGN) is a self-contained planner that keeps its own working copy of the executives, deliveries and queue in the browser (localStorage). When the DOODLY backend is reachable, the page overlays live data on top of it: the KPI cards, the Strategy selector and the ⚡ Auto-assign button all read and write the production database through /api/assignments/*. The green "● Live" banner at the top confirms this. The drag-drop board, Generate deliveries, Run tests and Reset act on the local planner only — they never touch live orders.

⚠️
Warning — the 45-bottle cap is an invariant

No executive is ever loaded past capacity, by any path — auto-assign, drag-drop, or return-trip pull. The engine guards this on every placement. A single order larger than the biggest capacity can never fit a trip and is parked in the queue with the reason oversize. Per-driver capacity can be raised or lowered on the Drivers screen (DeliveryCapacity); 45 is only the default.

Generate deliveries (planner only)

The Generate deliveries ▾ button opens a small menu offering 25 · 100 · 500 · 1000 synthetic deliveries. It appends that many confirmed test deliveries to the current slot in the local planner so you can rehearse a busy morning. It does not create real orders.

⚠️
Warning — Reset wipes the planner

Reset asks "Reset all executives, deliveries and the queue?" and, on confirm, clears the entire local planner back to its seed. This is a planner-only action — it does not delete anything from the database — but you will lose any manual drag-drop arrangement you had staged. Use it only to start a fresh rehearsal.

✅
Best Practice — the morning run

Before the 5:30 AM dispatch: (1) confirm executives are marked Available, (2) pick the correct Slot, (3) press ⚡ Auto-assign, (4) clear any Pending queue either by freeing more executives or by dragging chips manually, then (5) step each executive to Accepted → Out For Delivery as they leave. Aim to reach 0 unassigned orders before the vans roll.

ℹ️
Note — who can receive work

Auto-assign only loads executives whose status is Available or Returned To Dairy. Once an executive is Accepted, Out For Delivery, Offline or on Break they are skipped, and their in-flight deliveries are protected from re-assignment. The working set is captured once at the start of a pass, so an executive keeps filling toward 45 even after their badge flips to Assigned.

Why a delivery is queued

The engine tags every queued delivery with a reason so you know how to clear it.

ReasonMeaningHow to clear it
capacity_fullEvery available executive reached 45 bottles.Bring more executives on shift, or wait for one to return.
no_executiveNo executive was available at all.Mark an executive Available and re-run auto-assign.
oversizeThe single delivery is larger than the biggest capacity — it can never fit one trip.Split the order, or raise that driver's capacity on the Drivers screen.
locked_capacityThe delivery is locked to an executive who has no room.Unlock it, or free capacity on that executive.
manual_unassignAn admin unassigned it back to the queue (priority 1).Re-run auto-assign or drag it onto an executive.

Return-trip continuation

When an executive is stepped to Returned To Dairy, the system closes their trip and immediately tries to start a fresh one from the queue. It pulls queued deliveries preferring the executive's own zone, then nearest, then queue priority and FIFO order, up to the full 45-bottle capacity. If nothing fits, the executive simply goes back to Available.

Executive returnsTrip archived to historyExecutive freed to 0 bottlesQueue sorted (zone → nearest → priority → FIFO)Fill to 45New trip assigned + executive notified
Figure — an empty queue leaves the returned executive Available; a full queue starts a new trip automatically.
ℹ️
Note — nothing is ever lost

Overflow is captured, not dropped. In the live engine, DeliveryAssignment.deliveryId and AssignmentQueue.deliveryId are unique, so a delivery can appear in exactly one place — assigned or queued — and two admins clicking at once can never double-assign it. When queued bottles cross 50, admins get a "queue growing" alert; if there are no executives at all, they get a "no executives" alert.

⚠️
Warning — capacity and locks are enforced server-side

A manual move that would push the target past capacity is rejected with a 409 and a message like "Over capacity: needs 6, only 3 left." A locked assignment cannot be reassigned (unless forced) or unassigned. Both the executive who loses a delivery and the one who gains it are notified, and every move is written to the assignment log with your role attached.

✅
Best Practice — lock the fragile ones

For VIP customers, cold-chain-critical orders or a stop an executive knows personally, lock the chip after placing it. That guarantees the next auto-assign run and any queue pull will route around it instead of shuffling it to another van.

Assignment strategy — the distribution mode switch

Choose how the engine spreads work: evenly (Startup Mode), by capacity, by area, or fully manual.

Purpose Change how auto-assign distributes deliveries without a code deploy — the setting is stored in the database and applies to every future run.
Access Super Admin Admin — reading it needs deliveries:view; changing it needs deliveries:assign/edit and is audited. Path Admin → Operations → Auto Assignment → Strategy

ℹ️
Note — Manual mode reports honestly

With Manual selected, pressing ⚡ Auto-assign does nothing and returns "Manual mode — auto-assignment is switched off. Assign deliveries from the board." Nothing is queued or moved. Switch back to Equal or Capacity to resume automatic distribution.

💡
Tip — it is a global, sticky setting

The strategy lives in one AppSetting row (assignment.strategy), so it survives page reloads and applies for everyone until changed. Changing it is recorded in the audit trail as assignment.strategy.update with the old → new value, so you can always see who switched modes and when.

Where the data lives

The live engine reads and writes these tables inside race-safe, serialisable transactions. Attribution (who ran what) rides on your role, recorded on every log row.

Frequently asked questions

Why is capacity counted in bottles instead of customers?

Because the physical constraint of a delivery trip is how many glass bottles fit on the vehicle, not how many stops there are. One customer might take six bottles and another one — the engine packs to a 45-bottle ceiling so a van is never over-loaded regardless of how those bottles are split across stops.

Do I have to press Auto-assign again when an executive comes back?

No. Stepping an executive to Returned To Dairy automatically archives their finished trip and pulls the next batch from the queue for them — nearest-zone first, up to capacity. You only run auto-assign again to distribute newly confirmed orders or to drain the queue onto other free executives.

What is the difference between the "Assigned" and "Assigned orders" cards?

Assigned counts bottles already loaded onto executives; Assigned orders (a live-only card) counts deliveries. The pair, together with Completion %, lets you judge both the volume on the road and how many stops are still unplaced.

Is the drag-drop board changing real assignments?

The drag-drop arrangement is staged in the planner. Per-delivery override gestures (reassign / unassign / lock) are wired to /api/assignments/override and do change the live plan; the ⚡ Auto-assign button and the KPI/Strategy overlay are the other live paths. Generate deliveries, Run tests and Reset are always planner-only.
