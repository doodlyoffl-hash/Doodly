Auto Assignment

The morning control room that spreads every confirmed delivery across your delivery executives fairly and within their carrying capacity — automatically packing trips, queueing the overflow, re-filling executives the moment they return to the dairy, and letting you override any assignment by hand.

ℹ️
What "Auto Assignment" does at DOODLY

DOODLY delivers fresh A2 buffalo milk in glass bottles every morning. Each executive can physically carry only a limited number of bottles per trip — the system's hard cap is 45 bottles per trip (counted by bottles, not by number of customers). Auto Assignment takes today's confirmed deliveries for a time slot and distributes them across the executives who are free right now, keeping every trip at or under 45 bottles, grouping nearby stops together, and ordering each executive's route for the shortest drive. Anything that doesn't fit goes into a Pending Assignment Queue; when an executive finishes and returns to the dairy, the next batch is pulled from that queue for them. You can always step in and reassign, unassign, lock or move a delivery by hand.

ℹ️
Live engine vs on-screen planner

This screen has two layers working together. The on-screen board (the executive cards, queue and activity feed) is a fast visual planner that mirrors the assignment logic. The ⚡ Auto-assign button is wired to the real engine: it calls POST /api/assignments/auto against the DOODLY database, then refreshes the KPI tiles from GET /api/assignments/dashboard. When the backend is reachable you see a green banner — "● Live — auto-assignment engine on the DOODLY database …: N order(s), N executive(s) on trip, N queued". In production the board starts empty (a clean professional state) and fills only from real assignments; there are no fake executives or deliveries. This manual documents the live behaviour.

Warning — "Generate deliveries" and "Reset" are planner tools, not order tools

Generate deliveries creates practice deliveries on the board for rehearsing or load-testing the layout; it never places real customer orders. Reset wipes the on-screen board. Neither touches real orders, customers or money. The button that actually moves the database is ⚡ Auto-assign, which distributes the real confirmed deliveries for the day's slot.

ℹ️
Why a delivery lands in the queue

The backend tags each queued delivery with a reason: capacity_full (every free executive already hit 45), no_executive (no one is available at all), oversize (a single delivery bigger than one trip's capacity — it can never fit), locked_capacity (locked to an executive who has no room), or manual_unassign (you sent it back by hand). If more than 50 bottles pile up in the queue for a slot, admins and operations get an in-app alert — "Pending queue growing".

Activity & notifications

The bottom-right panel is a running feed of the latest eight events — auto-assign runs, status changes, queue pulls, reassigns, locks, and the notifications sent to executives and admins. It is your quick audit of what the board just did. On the live database, every assignment action is also written to the permanent AssignmentLog (actions AUTO_ASSIGN, QUEUE, DEQUEUE, MANUAL_ASSIGN, REASSIGN, UNASSIGN, RETURN_TRIP, LOCK, UNLOCK) with the actor's role.

✅
Best Practice — start on EQUAL, move to AREA as you scale

While the team is small and every executive should pull the same weight, keep Equal distribution (Startup). As delivery density grows and drive time starts to hurt, switch to Area based so nearby stops ride together. Only use Manual for one-off situations — and remember to switch back, or the next morning's ⚡ Auto-assign will silently do nothing.

ℹ️
Nobody sits idle

The moment an executive returns, the engine (POST /api/assignments/return) pulls the highest-priority waiting deliveries — preferring the same or nearest zone — and starts a fresh trip up to the 45-bottle cap. The executive gets a "New trip after returning" notification. If the queue is empty they simply go back to Available. This is what lets a handful of executives clear a large morning in waves.

ℹ️
Who gets notified

After a run, each executive who received a trip is notified ("New trip assigned · N bottles · N stops"). If no executives are available, admins/operations are alerted and everything is queued. If the queue passes 50 bottles, admins/operations get a "Pending queue growing" alert so someone can bring more executives on shift.

✅
Best Practice — the morning routine

Set the Slot to the morning window and confirm the Strategy is what you want for today. Bring every driver on shift to Available, then press ⚡ Auto-assign once. Read the KPI strip: if Pending queue is amber, either bring another executive online and press Assign queue to available execs, or hand-place the queued chips onto executives with room. As executives return, the queue re-fills them automatically — you only step in for exceptions. Use Lock on any delivery you have deliberately hand-placed so the next run leaves it alone, and keep Manual mode for special cases only.

Frequently asked questions

Q. Is capacity counted in bottles or in customers?

A. In bottles. The 45-per-trip cap is about what an executive can physically carry, so a single customer ordering many bottles uses more of the cap than several one-bottle customers. The capacity bar on each card always reads "bottles / 45".

Q. What is the difference between the on-screen board and the database?

A. The board is a live planner you can rehearse on (including "Generate deliveries" practice data). The ⚡ Auto-assign button is what commits real work: it runs the engine against the DOODLY database and writes real assignments, trips and logs. In production the board fills only from real assignments — it starts empty.

Q. If I press Auto-assign twice, will customers get duplicate trips?

A. No. The engine is idempotent and the database enforces one assignment per delivery, so a second press only picks up anything still unassigned — it never duplicates an existing trip.

Q. What happens when an executive returns to the dairy mid-morning?

A. Their finished trip is archived, they're freed to zero bottles, and the engine immediately pulls the next batch from the pending queue (nearest / same-zone first) to start a fresh trip up to 45 bottles. They get a "New trip after returning" notification. If the queue is empty they go back to Available.

Q. How do I stop the system from reshuffling a delivery I placed by hand?

A. Lock it. Click the 🔓 on the chip to lock it to its executive; auto-assignment then skips it. A locked delivery also can't be unassigned or reassigned until you unlock it (or force the move).

Q. Which time slots can I assign?

A. The board offers 5:30 AM, 7:00 AM, 11:30 AM and 5:00 PM. Only deliveries in the selected slot are assigned; the live engine runs against the day's morning window.
