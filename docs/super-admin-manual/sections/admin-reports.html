Reports

One live business-intelligence screen that computes every headline number, trend chart and drill-down table straight from the DOODLY database — sales, customers, subscriptions, finance, operations, procurement and marketing — for any date range, ready to export to CSV, Excel, PDF or print.

ℹ️
Note — this is a read-only analytics page

Reports never changes an order, payment, subscription or any other record. It reads the authoritative source tables (orders, subscriptions, payments, wallet, procurement, quality tests, deliveries, referrals, trial cashback, products and expenses) and re-computes the figures on demand. There are no separate "analytics" tables to keep in sync — every number you see is the true, current state of the business for the range you picked.

💡
How the data reaches this screen

Opening Admin → Growth → Reports calls the backend endpoint GET /api/admin/reports?preset=…. The server resolves the preset to a date range, aggregates the source tables live, and returns the KPIs, chart series and category tables in one payload. A green "● Live — analytics computed from the DOODLY database" banner confirms the source and the exact range. Every filter change re-fetches; nothing is cached client-side.

Note — daily vs. monthly charts switch automatically

The trend charts adapt to the range you pick. If the span is 45 days or shorter the bars are drawn one-per-day; anything longer (Quarter, Financial yr, All time) is bucketed into the last six months of monthly bars. ⚠️
Warning — several KPIs are lifetime, not range-scoped

Read the labels carefully. Total Revenue, Total Orders, Completed Orders, Active Subscriptions, Renewal/Retention Rate, Avg Order Value, Wallet Usage, Referral Growth, Procurement Cost, GST Collected, Gross Profit and Net Profit are computed over the whole business history, not the chip you selected. Only New Customers and the trend charts / sales table follow the chosen range. This is by design — the KPI strip is a "state of the business" summary; use the charts, the daily table and the drill-downs for range-specific analysis.

ℹ️
Note — "Gross Profit" and "Net Profit" are proxies

Gross profit currently treats milk procurement cost as the only cost of goods sold, and net profit additionally subtracts approved operating expenses (expenses that are not REJECTED or CANCELLED and not soft-deleted). These are honest, useful directional figures but not a full P&L; treat them as management indicators, not audited accounts.

Warning — allow pop-ups for PDF / Print

Export PDF and Print open a new browser window. If your browser blocks pop-ups the screen shows "Allow pop-ups to pdf/print" and nothing downloads. Allow pop-ups for the admin site once and both work every time. CSV and Excel download directly and are never blocked.

✅
Best Practice — pick the range before you export

The exports and the daily table always reflect the currently selected chip, so choose your period first (for example "Financial yr" for a year-end pack), let the board reload, then click Export. For a board-meeting summary, "This month" or "Quarter" gives clean monthly bars; for finance reconciliation, "Financial yr" aligns with the April–March books.

Frequently asked questions

Q. Do these numbers match the Revenue and Finance screens exactly?

They are computed from the same source tables, so revenue, GST and order counts line up. Reports is the cross-functional summary; the dedicated Admin → Growth → Revenue screen adds a full transaction ledger and revenue-recognition breakdowns for accountants.

Q. Can I choose a custom start and end date?

The screen offers the nine preset chips. The backend endpoint itself accepts explicit from and to parameters, but the standard board UI drives it through the presets — pick the closest preset for on-screen analysis, and export if you need to reshape the period offline.

Q. Why is "Net Profit" different from our accountant's figure?

Net profit here is a management proxy: revenue minus milk procurement cost minus approved operating expenses. It does not include payroll, depreciation, taxes beyond GST collected, or other book adjustments. Use it to watch the trend, not to close the books.

Q. Are cancelled or pending orders counted in revenue?

No. Revenue, GST, average order value and the sales tables count only orders with status PAID. Pending and other statuses still appear in the "Orders by status" breakdown and the "Total Orders" count, but they contribute no revenue.
