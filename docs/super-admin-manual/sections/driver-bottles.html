Bottle Collection

The delivery executive's task list for picking up empty glass bottles along today's route — a count-and-confirm screen whose totals feed straight into each customer's bottle ledger and reward points.

ℹ️
Where this screen sits

Bottle Collection is a page inside the Delivery Executive portal (surface driver), not the admin console. Its manifest key is driver/bottles and it lives at /driver/bottles.html. The executive reaches it from the driver navigation under Tasks. A Super Admin normally looks at this screen to understand what an executive sees in the field, or while helping an executive over the phone; the same numbers reconcile against the customer's /account/bottles.html ledger and the warehouse view under Admin → Catalogue → Bottle Inventory. This is a reference and count screen — the empties count is actually written when the executive marks the stop delivered (see the Workflow).

This page shares the route stop list

The table on Bottle Collection is the same driverStops dataset used by Today's Route and Deliveries — it is declared tbl("driverStops",{toolbar:false,pager:false}). It therefore has no dedicated "bottles" column: it is a cross-reference of the day's stops so the executive can see which customer is next. The actual per-stop empties count is entered on the Delivery Detail / mark-delivered step, which is where the number is captured and saved (documented in Screen 2). Think of this page as "how many, and where", and the delivery step as "record what I collected here".

The four KPI cards

The KPI strip is the kpis block with four hard-declared items in the manifest. In a live signed-in session those figures are the executive's real day totals, computed server-side by GET /api/driver/summary from every Delivery assigned to this driver for today.

⚠️
Warning — "Damaged" is not an automatic counter

The backend bottle ledger tracks five events — ISSUED, RETURNED, LOST, DEPOSIT_CHARGED, DEPOSIT_REFUNDED — and the driver summary returns only "to collect" and "collected today". There is no separate "damaged" tally. The Damaged KPI is a placeholder that stays at 0 unless a damaged bottle is explicitly recorded through the stop's issue report (the "Damaged bottle" reason) and later written off as a LOST ledger row by operations. Do not read a non-zero "Damaged" figure as an audited financial number — trace it to the issue report and the LOST row.

ℹ️
Bottles out vs. bottles in

bottlesOut = the full bottles you delivered (the customer receives them → an ISSUED row, defaulting to the stop's bottleCount). bottlesIn = the empties you picked up (the customer returns them → a RETURNED row). Bottle Collection is about the bottlesIn side: how many empties came back.

⛔
Caution — the count is final once you confirm the stop

Marking the stop delivered writes the RETURNED row to the customer's append-only ledger and stamps deliveredAt. The ledger is never edited in place — a wrong count can only be corrected by posting a new, opposite entry through operations. Count the empties in front of the customer and set the stepper correctly before you tap "Mark delivered".

ℹ️
You can only touch your own stops

The endpoint first resolves the Driver record for the signed-in user, then loads the delivery where driverId matches that driver. A stop that belongs to another executive returns "Delivery not found on your route." A user with no linked delivery profile gets a 403 "No delivery profile is linked to this account."

Operating notes

Best practice, troubleshooting and common questions for Bottle Collection.

✅
Best Practice

Work the route in sequence and clear the Remaining KPI to zero before you head back to the hub. Because "To collect" is the sum of expected empties over un-finished stops, any bottle you forget to log stays counted against the customer forever (an ISSUED with no matching RETURNED) and inflates their pending-bottle balance. If a customer genuinely has no empty to give today, still mark the stop delivered with bottlesIn = 0 so the day reconciles cleanly.

Frequently asked questions

Q. What is the difference between "bottles out" and "bottles in"?

Bottles out are the full bottles you deliver — they create an ISSUED row and default to the stop's planned bottleCount. Bottles in are the empty bottles you collect back — they create a RETURNED row. Bottle Collection is about the "in" side: how many empties came home.

Q. Does the customer get anything when I collect their empties?

Yes. On delivery the system awards DOODLY Pure Rewards points for the bottles returned (earn.bottleReturn), and a 200-point bonus every 12 consecutive delivered stops on their subscription. That is why an accurate empties count matters to the customer, not just to inventory.

Q. Can I fix a wrong empties count after I've marked the stop delivered?

Not from the field — the bottle ledger is append-only. Ask operations to post an offsetting entry (for example a RETURNED to correct an under-count). This is why you should confirm the number with the customer before tapping "Mark delivered".

Q. How do these numbers reach the customer and the warehouse?

The RETURNED / ISSUED rows you post appear immediately on the customer's /account/bottles.html ledger (their Pending and Deposit figures recompute from all-time totals), and the physical fleet is reconciled by operations under Admin → Catalogue → Bottle Inventory.

Q. Where do damaged or lost bottles go?

They are not part of the clean "collected" count. Raise the stop's issue report with the "Damaged bottle" reason; if the bottle is written off, operations record it as a LOST ledger event, which reduces the customer's pending bottles without counting as a return.
