Delivery Management

The daily control room for milk deliveries — see who is delivering what, dispatch the day's route in one click, assign or reassign a delivery executive, move an order through its delivery stages, and log any problem in the field.

ℹ️
Note

This screen reads and writes the live DOODLY database. Every number on the page is calculated from real Delivery records — it is not a demo sheet. When the backend is reachable, a green banner appears below the toolbar reading "● Live — N delivery record(s) from the DOODLY database". If the backend is offline or your role lacks permission, a red banner explains why.

ℹ️
Note — fixed slot

From this screen the dispatch always targets today and the 06:00–08:00 morning slot (DOODLY's core delivery window). To dispatch a different date or slot, or to drag-and-drop individual stops, use the Auto Assignment screen.

⚠️
Warning — Manual strategy

If the assignment strategy is set to Manual, pressing Generate does nothing automatic — the response says "Manual mode — auto-assignment is switched off" and reports 0 assigned. Switch the strategy on the Auto Assignment screen first, or assign each delivery by hand from the list below.

Best Practice

Read the KPIs top-to-bottom each morning: Delayed and Pending tell you what to fix now; Bottle collection and Customer rating tell you how yesterday's service actually landed. A steadily falling bottle-collection % usually means executives are skipping the empties step, not that customers aren't returning bottles.

The Bottles column

Bottles reads as collected / total — for example 2/3 means the executive has recorded two empty bottles back out of three expected for that stop. It comes from the record's bottlesIn and bottleCount.

Where the rows come from

Each row is a Delivery record. The Customer is taken from the linked subscription (or one-off order); Zone / area is the delivery address's city and pincode; Driver is the assigned executive's name, or "—" when unassigned. The executive updates status and bottle counts from their mobile delivery app; this admin list is the mirror.

⚠️
Warning — assigning is a privileged action

Setting or changing the Delivery executive requires the full-level deliveries:assign special. If your role only has "manage" on deliveries you can still change the Status, but the save will return "Your role can't assign/update deliveries (403)" the moment it detects a driver change. Operations Managers, Admins and Super Admins hold the full level.

ℹ️
Note — issue needs an executive first

You cannot log an issue on an unassigned delivery. Assign an executive and save first; then the Log issue action will succeed. This keeps every issue attributed to a specific driver.

✅
Best Practice

Use Status overrides sparingly — normally the executive drives status from their mobile app. Reach for the manual override only to correct a mistake (e.g. mark a wrongly-failed stop back to Scheduled for a re-attempt), and always add an issue note so the reason is on the record.

Frequently asked questions

Delivery Management.

Does "Generate deliveries" send anything to customers?

No. It assigns deliveries to executives and notifies the executives of their new trips. Customer-facing messages are handled elsewhere (order and delivery notifications).

What's the difference between this screen and Auto Assignment?

Delivery Management is the day's dashboard and record list with a one-click dispatch for today's morning slot. Auto Assignment is the full engine — pick any date/slot, change the strategy, watch live capacity, and drag stops between executives. Both write to the same records.

Why can I change a delivery's status but not its executive?

Changing status needs deliveries:edit (the "manage" level). Assigning or moving an executive is a higher-trust action that needs the full-level deliveries:assign special. Super Admin, Admin and Operations Manager have both.

How many deliveries does the list show?

Up to 150 records, newest date first then route sequence. Use search and the status filter chips to narrow down; the DataTable engine paginates the rest.

What does 45 mean in the dispatch?

Each executive can carry up to 45 glass bottles per trip. The engine packs trips up to that cap; anything that doesn't fit lands in the pending queue and is picked up when an executive returns to the dairy.
