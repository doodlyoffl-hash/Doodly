Business Invoices

A premium, print-ready statement of account for every B2B partner — supply analytics, financial summary, an outstanding-and-credit tracker and payment history — backed by an automated engine that issues numbered GST invoices per B2B order and emails a branded PDF to the buyer.

ℹ️
Two things live under one name

"Business Invoices" is made of two real pieces of code that work together:

1 — The screen you open at Admin → Commerce → Business Invoices (/admin/invoice-b2b.html). The page's invoiceB2B block (registered in blocks.js) mounts a Business Partner Statement built by invoice.js → DOODLY_INVOICE.mountB2B. On load, layout.js → wireB2BInvoiceBackend() pulls the real businesses and orders from the DOODLY database and re-renders the statement from them.

2 — The invoice engine in the Next.js backend (next-app/lib/b2b/invoices.ts, invoice-email.ts, invoice-pdf.ts behind /api/b2b/invoices). This issues the actual numbered BusinessInvoice records, auto-emails the branded PDF, tracks delivery, and records an append-only audit trail. Invoices are generated from the B2B Orders module ("Generate invoice"); this section documents both the statement you read and the engine that powers it.

Business Partner Statement

The full statement of account for one B2B partner — the screen this page opens on.

Purpose Give the office one printable, shareable document per business that summarises what was supplied, what was billed, what is outstanding, how much credit is left, and the recent payment history — so a partner's account can be reviewed or sent in seconds.
Access Super Admin Admin — the underlying B2B data endpoints are Admin/Super-Admin only (canUseB2B in lib/b2b/guard.ts). No other staff role can load it. Path Admin → Commerce → Business Invoices

The page header reads "Business Partner Statement — Premium B2B statement: supply analytics, financial summary, outstanding tracker, payment history and credit insights. Print, PDF, Excel, email or WhatsApp." Below it, a single business is shown as a full statement document. A Business picker at the top switches between partners; choosing another business rewrites the page's ?id= and re-draws the whole statement for that partner. Money is animated with a count-up on open (animations are switched off for print and under "reduce motion").

Live wiring & the "● Live" banner

When the backend is reachable, wireB2BInvoiceBackend() fetches /api/b2b/businesses and /api/b2b/orders, mirrors them into the store the statement reads, re-mounts the statement, and shows a green banner: "● Live — B2B statement from the DOODLY database (N business(es), M order(s))." If the backend is offline the banner reads "⚠ Backend offline…"; if your role is blocked it reads "⚠ Your role can't access B2B (403)." The statement's identity, the selected business's profile (order count, revenue, average daily, outstanding, preferred products) and the payment-history rows are drawn from that business's real registered profile and recorded B2B orders.

How the totals relate

The statement enforces the accounting identity Total outstanding = previous outstanding + current invoice − payments received − credit notes − adjustments, and Available credit = credit limit − outstanding (never below zero). These same relationships are unit-tested inside invoice.js (runTests()).

Best Practice

Set the branding, terms and signature once, before the first real invoice goes out. Because these settings also drive the customer invoice and are baked into every emailed PDF, changing them mid-cycle means older documents and newer ones won't match. Use the Watermark ("DRAFT") while you are still testing, and clear it before real billing.

Business Invoice engine & automated email

The backend that issues numbered GST invoices per B2B order and emails the branded PDF.

Purpose Turn a delivered B2B order into a permanent, numbered BusinessInvoice record; auto-email the buyer a premium branded email with the PDF attached; track delivery, payments and status; and keep an append-only audit trail — all money-safe and idempotent.
Access Super Admin Admin for every endpoint (canUseB2B); resend email is Super Admin only. The emailed PDF/View links carry a signed token so the buyer can open their own invoice without logging in. Path Admin → Commerce → B2B Orders → Generate invoice · API /api/b2b/invoices
ℹ️
Where invoices are created

An invoice is issued from a B2B order — either automatically as part of the order flow, or by POST /api/b2b/invoices with an orderId. Each order can have exactly one invoice (a second attempt is rejected: "This order already has an invoice."). Amounts and line items always derive from the order; payments come from the order's BusinessPayment records.

Automated email — status & safeguards

When an invoice is created the engine automatically builds the PDF and sends a premium branded email to the business's address, then records the outcome on the invoice's Email status:

Email statusMeaningWhat happens
SENTThe email was delivered to the provider.Records message id + sent-at; increments retry count; logs an "email" event. Won't re-send unless a Super Admin forces a resend (idempotent).
PENDINGThe email provider isn't configured (e.g. local dev without a Resend key).Queued quietly, no failure noise; sends once the provider is set.
SKIPPEDThe business has no email on file.Marks skipped and alerts Super Admins in-app to add an address and resend.
FAILEDSending failed after retries.Records the error, alerts Super Admins, and can be retried from the invoice.
ℹ️
Graceful degradation

If the PDF fails to generate, the email is still sent with a secure download link instead of an attachment, and Super Admins are alerted. The View/Download links in the email are signed and valid for 60 days, so the buyer can open the invoice without an account. The whole send flow never throws into the order/invoice code — a failed email never blocks issuing the invoice.

Invoice reports (GET /api/b2b/invoices/reports)

A date-ranged roll-up across all live (non-void) invoices: total invoices, count by status, invoiced revenue, collected, outstanding, GST, overdue count and overdue value, plus a by-business breakdown (top 10 by revenue, each with count, revenue and outstanding).

Is the money on the statement live?

The partner's identity, profile (order count, revenue, average daily volume, outstanding, preferred products) and the payment-history rows come from the selected business's real registered record and recorded B2B orders. The statement enforces the standard accounting identities (total outstanding, available credit) shown above. For a strictly per-invoice, database-authoritative figure, open the numbered invoice via the engine (Screen 3).

What's the difference between the statement's "Download PDF" and the engine's PDF?

The statement's Download PDF prints the on-screen statement through the browser. The engine's PDF (/api/b2b/invoices/[id]/pdf) is a server-rendered vector PDF of a single numbered invoice, built with pdf-lib and used as the email attachment — that's the document you send to a partner for their accounts.

Who receives the automated invoice email?

The business's email on file. The email carries the branded invoice, a totals summary, and signed View/Download links valid for 60 days. Delivery is tracked on the invoice; if it fails or is skipped, Super Admins get an in-app alert to fix and resend.

✅
Best Practice

Before month-end billing, open each partner's statement, confirm the credit limit and due date are right, and clear any red alerts. Then issue invoices from B2B Orders so every partner gets the automated branded email + PDF. Reconcile the /api/b2b/invoices/reports outstanding and overdue totals against your ledger at the close of each cycle.
