Daily Expenses

The books-of-account for everything DOODLY spends — milk procurement, farmer payments, fuel, salaries, bottles, rent, bills and more — recorded with a supplier and bill number, sent through an approve → pay workflow, backed by attachments and a full audit trail, and rolled up into dashboards and exportable reports.

ℹ️
One module, two implementations, identical rules

The Daily Expenses item under Admin → Finance is served by the storefront admin page (/admin/expenses.html), which mounts the DOODLY_EXPENSES module into #expensesMount. It presents five working tabs — Dashboard, Expenses, Add Expense, Categories, Reports — that record and manage every business expense. The same feature set is mirrored by a production-grade Next.js API (/api/expenses) whose engine, validation, statuses, categories and workflow match the screen exactly. This manual documents the complete capability set. Money is entered and shown in ₹; the backend stores every amount as integer paise (₹1 = 100 paise). Every action — create, edit, approve, reject, pay, cancel, delete — appends an immutable line to the expense's audit trail.

ℹ️
Finance-only, with separation of duties

Only three roles can open this module at all: Accountant, Admin and Super Admin. An Accountant may view, create, edit, record payments and run reports — but may not approve, reject, cancel, delete or manage categories. Those are reserved for Admin and Super Admin. This is a deliberate separation of duties: the accountant who records an expense is not the person who approves it. Server-side role checks (canUseExpenses, canApproveExpenses, canManageExpenseSettings) are the real security boundary; the on-screen hiding of buttons is convenience only.

ℹ️
How an expense is numbered

Every expense gets a human-readable, never-reused code of the form EXP-YYYYMMDD-NNNN — the expense date, then a four-digit per-day sequence. The first expense dated 1 Jul 2026 is EXP-20260701-0001, the next that day EXP-20260701-0002, and so on. The backend allocates the sequence atomically from a Counter so two people saving at the same moment never collide on a number.

ℹ️
Rejected and cancelled spend never counts

Every dashboard number is computed over "live" spend only — expenses whose status is Rejected or Cancelled are excluded from all totals and charts, and soft-deleted expenses disappear entirely. This keeps the KPIs honest: a rejected bill never inflates "This Month".

⚠️
Approve before you pay

A payment can only be recorded against an Approved or Partially Paid expense — the server rejects "Expense must be approved before recording a payment." on anything still Pending Approval. The workflow is intentional: request → approve → pay. Likewise a Paid, Rejected or Cancelled expense is terminal and can no longer be edited.

ℹ️
Separation of duties is enforced server-side

The split is not just a UI nicety — the API checks canUseExpenses to open the module, then canApproveExpenses (Admin / Super Admin only) before approve, reject, cancel or delete, and canManageExpenseSettings before category changes. An Accountant who tries an approver-only action gets "Only an Admin or Super Admin can perform this action." Support, operations, procurement, quality, delivery and marketing roles have no expense access at all.

✅
Best Practice — file the bill with the expense, and let approval gate payment

When you record an expense, attach the actual invoice or receipt in the Supporting documents section and fill in the Vendor and Invoice/bill number — those three make the vendor-wise report and any later audit trivial. Let the workflow do its job: create as Pending Approval, have a different person (an Admin) Approve, and only then Record payment. Use Partially Paid honestly when a supplier is paid in instalments; the outstanding figure on the dashboard and reports depends on it. When something is wrong, prefer Reject (with a reason) or Cancel over Delete — a soft-delete hides the row but a rejected/cancelled expense keeps a visible, reasoned trail.

Frequently asked questions

Q. What's the difference between "Amount" and "Total"?

Amount is the base you type. Total is what the expense actually costs: when "GST included?" is off, Total = Amount + GST; when it's on, the GST is already inside so Total = Amount (the GST figure is shown for information). The list, dashboard and reports all use the Total.

Q. Can I pay a supplier in instalments?

Yes. On an Approved expense, use the Record payment box to add each instalment with its amount and mode. The status becomes Partially Paid and the Outstanding figure drops with each payment; once the payments reach the total it flips to Paid automatically. Or use Mark fully paid to settle the whole balance at once.

Q. I filed an expense against the wrong category — how do I fix it?

Open the expense, click Edit, change the Category and save — as long as it isn't Paid/Rejected/Cancelled. The change is logged as an "updated" audit line. If you also want to remove the old category, you can only delete it once no expense uses it.

Q. What happens when I delete an expense?

It's a soft delete — the expense is hidden from every list, total and report, but it is never physically removed; the backend just stamps deletedAt and keeps the record (and its audit trail) for integrity. There is no hard delete in this module.

Q. Who shows up in the "Requested / Approved / Paid by" pickers?

Active staff only — the backend lists non-customer, non-delivery-executive users. Customers and delivery executives never appear, because they don't handle expenses.

Q. Are the dashboard and report numbers "real time"?

Yes — they recompute from the current expense data every time you open the tab, always excluding rejected, cancelled and soft-deleted rows. There's no cache to refresh.
