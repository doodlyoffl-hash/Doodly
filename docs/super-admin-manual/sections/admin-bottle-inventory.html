Bottle Inventory

Track DOODLY's reusable glass-bottle fleet across its full lifecycle — from sanitised stock, out on delivery, back for collection and cleaning, to damaged or lost — and see the customer deposits the business is holding against those bottles.

ℹ️
Why this screen exists

Every DOODLY bottle is a reusable asset the company owns, not packaging that is thrown away. A single bottle is issued to a customer, returned empty, washed, and sent out again many times. Bottle Inventory is the asset register for that fleet: it counts how many bottles sit in each stage, warns when too many are stuck with customers, and keeps an append-only ledger of every movement so the physical count and the books always agree.

The KPI tiles and the ledger below are populated the moment the page opens by two backend calls — GET /api/admin/bottles/overview (the counters, notice and fleet matrix) and GET /api/admin/bottles/movements?pageSize=100 (the ledger). Until those return, the tiles show demo placeholder numbers; the green banner confirms when live database figures have replaced them.

ℹ️
Two views of the same fleet

The backend keeps two coupled records. The bottle stock side counts physical bottles by capacity and stage (the six tiles above). The customer ledger side records per-customer Issued / Returned / Lost events and the deposit money. "Pending return" and "Deposits held" come from the customer ledger; the other four tiles come from the physical stock.

The amber notice bar

Directly under the tiles sits a warning banner. On a live page it is rewritten to read, for example: "418 bottles awaiting collection · 6,722 in circulation · fleet utilisation 85%." Fleet utilisation is the share of owned bottles that are out working — (In circulation + Awaiting collection) ÷ Total owned, as a percentage. A high figure means most of the fleet is in the field; a rising "awaiting collection" count is the cue to run a collection-reminder campaign.

Best Practice

Read the KPI tiles top-to-bottom before your morning dispatch. If In sanitised stock is running thin while Pending return and In circulation are high, the fleet is cash-flow-starved of clean bottles — prioritise a collection round or clean a backlog rather than buying new glass.

💡
Tip — the natural bottle cycle

The recommended path a bottle follows is: Available → In circulation → Awaiting collection → Cleaning → Available, looping forever. Damaged and Lost are the exits. The dialog lets you post any transition (physical reality varies), but staying on the natural cycle keeps the counts easy to reconcile.

⚠️
Warning — you cannot move more than you have

If you try to move more bottles out of a stage than it holds, the save is rejected with, for example, "Only 12 1000 ml bottle(s) in Available — cannot move 24." No stage can ever go negative. When that happens, count the physical bottles and correct the earlier movement by posting a fresh, opposite one — never assume the number is wrong.

⛔
Caution — movements are permanent

The ledger is append-only. There is no edit or delete. A movement you post cannot be undone from the screen — the only correction is to record a compensating movement in the other direction, with a reason that explains the fix. Marking bottles Lost removes them from "Total bottles owned"; recover them only by recording a Lost → Available movement.

✅
Best Practice

Write reasons a colleague could audit months later. "Empties collected on route R3, driver Ramesh" beats "collected". The reason and note are searchable in the ledger, so specific text pays off during stock reconciliation.

Frequently asked questions

Q. What is the difference between "In sanitised stock" and "Available"?

They are the same thing. The KPI tile is labelled "In sanitised stock" for readability, but it counts the AVAILABLE stage — clean bottles ready to go out.

Q. Why doesn't "Total bottles owned" include lost bottles?

A lost bottle is written off — the company no longer has it, so counting it as "owned" would overstate the fleet. Lost bottles appear only in the "Lost / damaged" tile. If a lost bottle turns up, record a Lost → Available movement and it re-joins the owned count.

Q. Do I need a customer to record a movement?

No. This screen moves the fleet in bulk by capacity and stage — it is the warehouse/asset view. Per-customer issue/return and deposit tracking live in the customer bottle ledger (surfaced on the customer's account and in "Pending return" / "Deposits held").

Q. Who shows up in the "By" column?

The role of whoever recorded the movement (for example, admin or inventory). The system also stores the actor's ID and IP address behind the scenes for the audit trail, though only the role is shown in the table.
