Revenue

One live revenue-analytics and revenue-recognition screen: eighteen headline KPI cards, a revenue-trend chart, three money breakdowns (by source, by payment method, by segment) and a full, searchable transaction ledger — every figure recognised on demand from the authoritative DOODLY database, ready to export to CSV, Excel or PDF.

ℹ️
Note — Revenue is read-only, and it has no tables of its own

The Revenue screen never edits an order, a payment or a wallet. It recognises revenue by reading the real source tables — orders and their items, payments and payment records, the wallet ledger, B2B business orders, invoices and payment refunds — and computes every total on the fly. A "revenue record" is simply a projection of an Order; there is no duplicate "revenue" table to keep in sync, so every number is the true, current state of the business.

💡
How the data reaches this screen

Opening Admin → Growth → Revenue calls GET /api/admin/revenue?view=dashboard&preset=… for the KPIs and charts, and GET /api/admin/revenue?view=records&… for the ledger. Clicking a row calls GET /api/admin/revenue?view=detail&id=…. A green "● Live — revenue recognised from the DOODLY database" banner confirms the source URL and the resolved date range. Every filter change re-fetches; nothing is cached in the browser.

Note — daily vs. monthly bars, and how "All time" behaves

The revenue-trend chart switches automatically: if the resolved span is 45 days or shorter the bars are drawn one per day; anything longer (Quarter, Financial yr) is bucketed into the last six months of monthly bars. Because All time sends no explicit start/end to the dashboard, the trend chart falls back to a last-30-day daily window even though the ledger below shows the full history. The KPI cards are lifetime regardless (see the warning under the KPI strip).

Warning — the KPI cards are lifetime totals, not range-scoped

With the exception of the four period cards (Today / This Week / This Month / This Year), every KPI card is computed over the whole business history and does not change when you click a preset chip. The preset chip re-scopes only the revenue-trend chart and the ledger table below. This is by design — the strip is a "state of the business" summary. Use the trend chart, the ledger filters and the exports for period-specific analysis.

ℹ️
Note — "B2B Revenue" is money collected, not money billed

The B2B Revenue card shows the total paid amount across business orders (paidPaise). What has been invoiced but not yet paid is tracked separately as outstanding B2B — you will find the billed-vs-collected picture under Admin → B2B → Invoices and Admin → Finance. MRR is a proxy (this month's subscription revenue) and ARR is simply that figure multiplied by twelve; treat both as directional indicators, not booked contract values.

✅
Best Practice — pick the range and filters before you export

The CSV/Excel/PDF exports include the ledger with the currently applied preset, source, status, sort and search. Choose your period and narrow the ledger first — for example "Financial yr" plus source "Milk Subscription" — let the board reload, then click Export. Because the server returns at most 200 ledger rows per export, tighten the filters for a large period rather than exporting "All time" in one shot.

Tip — the "Revenue ID" is a display code derived from the order

Each row's ID reads REV-XXXXXXXX, formed from the last eight characters of the underlying order ID (upper-cased). Search matches the customer name, phone, or invoice number most reliably. When you open a record its detail modal shows the same REV- code, so you can cross-reference it against an invoice or a payment.

Note — the ledger lists all order statuses, not just PAID

Unlike the revenue KPIs (which count only PAID orders), the ledger is a full order projection and will list PENDING, REFUNDED and FAILED rows too, so you can audit them. Their value is still classified correctly in the KPI strip — pending value under "Pending Payments", refunds under "Refunds" — even though the row appears here.

Filtering, refreshing, opening a record and exporting each send a POST to /api/admin/revenue that writes to the audit trail — actions revenue.filtered, revenue.refreshed, revenue.generated and revenue.exported (with the format captured). ✅
Best Practice — reconcile Revenue against Payments and Reports monthly

Revenue, Payments and Reports all read the same source tables, so their headline figures line up. Once a month, cross-check Net Revenue here against the Admin → Finance → Payments collected total and the Admin → Growth → Reports financial drill-down. If they diverge, look for orders stuck in PENDING (in the ledger) or refunds captured against a payment but not reflected in an order status.

Frequently asked questions

Q. What exactly counts as "revenue" on this screen?

Recognised revenue is the value of orders with status PAID (their totalPaise), plus collected B2B business orders. Pending orders are shown separately as "Pending Payments", and refunds are subtracted to give "Net Revenue". A pending or failed order can appear in the ledger for auditing, but it contributes no recognised revenue.

Q. Why is B2B Revenue lower than what we invoiced this month?

The B2B Revenue card shows money actually collected (paidPaise), not billed. The difference — billed but unpaid — is B2B outstanding, visible on the B2B Invoices and Finance screens. Revenue deliberately recognises cash collected, not credit extended.

Q. Are MRR and ARR real contract figures?

No. MRR is a proxy — the current calendar month's subscription revenue — and ARR is MRR × 12. They are useful trend indicators but are not derived from active-subscription contract values, so do not use them as booked recurring revenue.

Q. Do the Revenue numbers match the Reports and Payments screens?

Yes — all three read the same authoritative tables. Reports is the cross-functional summary, Payments is the gateway/settlement view, and Revenue adds the transaction ledger and the gross-to-net recognition breakdown that finance needs. Small wording differences aside, revenue, GST and order counts reconcile.

Q. Can I choose a custom start and end date?

The board is driven by the nine preset chips. The underlying endpoint also accepts explicit from and to parameters, but the standard UI uses presets — pick the closest one for on-screen analysis, then export if you need to reshape the exact period offline.
