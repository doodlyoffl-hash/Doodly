Delivery Executive — My Route

The mobile-first field app a delivery executive opens each morning to see today's assigned stops, navigate to every door, mark each delivery through its stages, collect empty bottles, and report any problem — with the customer and the ops team kept in sync the whole time.

ℹ️
What this module is

"My Route" is a separate, self-contained logistics app that lives under its own URL space (/delivery/*) with its own login. It is not part of the office Admin console — it is the phone screen the executive uses on the road. A Super Admin should understand it end-to-end because everything the executive taps here writes straight into the same Delivery, BottleLedger, DeliveryIssue and audit records the office team manages under Admin → Operations → Delivery Mgmt. This section documents exactly what the executive sees and does so you can train drivers and diagnose field issues accurately.

⚠️
Strict per-executive scoping

An executive only ever sees their own route. The backend (GET /api/delivery/my-route) filters deliveries by the driver record linked to the signed-in user, and every stop update is re-checked — updating a stop that is not on your route returns 403 "That stop isn't on your route." The role must be delivery_executive (a super_admin may open it for testing). On the live site, anyone who is not a signed-in executive is redirected to /delivery/login.html.

The hero header

At the top the app greets the executive by first name — "Good morning, {FirstName} 👋" — taken from the signed-in driver's own name. The sub-line reads the route name or code, then the date, then the stop count, for example "Central Morning · RT-07 · 2026-07-12 · 6 stops". When today has no assignments the app automatically shows the most recent day that does and appends "(latest assigned day)" so the screen is never blank for an active executive. If no route is assigned at all the sub-line reads "No route assigned yet".

The availability (shift) pill

On the right of the hero, when the executive is a real signed-in driver, a tappable pill shows the current shift state, read from GET /api/driver/availability. Tapping it flips the shift via POST /api/driver/availability.

Pill stateMeaningTap doesBackend
🟢 Available for assignment (green)On shift — the assignment engine may hand you new stopsEnds the shiftSets ExecutiveStatus.availability = OFFLINE
⚪ Not available — start shift (grey)Off shift — you will not receive auto-assignmentsStarts the shiftSets ExecutiveStatus.availability = AVAILABLE
⚠️
You cannot go off-shift mid-trip

If the executive is in the middle of a trip (backend availability is ASSIGNED, ACCEPTED or OUT_FOR_DELIVERY), the shift toggle is blocked with 409 "You have an active trip — complete your deliveries (or mark returned to dairy) before changing availability." Only executives whose status is AVAILABLE are eligible for new auto-assignments, so an executive who forgets to start their shift will simply receive no work. Every shift change is written to the AssignmentLog as a STATUS_CHANGE entry for the audit trail.

ℹ️
The pill only appears when the live status loads

If the availability call fails or the account has no executive profile, the pill is simply hidden — only the plain "Live" / "On shift" badge shows. No pill does not mean "off shift"; it means the shift status could not be read.

Live GPS reporting

Once the route is started (or if the page is reloaded while a trip is already in progress), the app quietly reports the executive's position to POST /api/delivery/location immediately and then every 30 seconds. This updates Driver.lat / lng / lastSeenAt, which is what powers the moving driver dot on both the admin route view and the customer's live tracking map. Reporting stops automatically once every stop on the route is delivered. If the phone denies location permission, the app silently skips reporting — nothing else breaks.

ℹ️
Why the customer sees the truck move

The executive does not "share location" as a separate action. Simply starting the route turns on GPS reporting, and the coordinates flow to the customer's tracking screen and the admin map. Ask executives to keep location permission granted so customers can watch their milk approach.

How the backend maps delivery status to the four stages

The card's progress bar is driven by the delivery's real status, simplified to four executive-facing stages: SCHEDULED / ASSIGNED / ACCEPTED / PACKED → Assigned; OUT_FOR_DELIVERY / ON_THE_WAY → On the way; REACHED → Reached; DELIVERED / SKIPPED → Delivered; a FAILED delivery falls back to Assigned so it can be re-attempted.

Tip — "Scooter" uses two-wheeler routing

The Scooter link opens Google Maps in two-wheeler mode, which prefers two-wheeler-friendly roads and gives more realistic ETAs than car routing. Encourage executives to pick the mode that matches their actual vehicle for accurate timing.

✅
Best practice — start the shift, then start the route

Train executives to do two taps at the depot: first tap the availability pill to go 🟢 Available (so the assignment engine can still hand them last-minute stops), then tap Start route once loaded. Starting the route both moves every stop to "On the way" and turns on the live GPS trail that customers rely on. Skipping "Start route" leaves customers with no tracking dot.

What "Mark delivered" does

Confirming sends POST /api/delivery/stop/{id} with action = deliver, the bottle count and any note. In a single database transaction the backend:

StepEffect
1. Close the stopSets Delivery.status = DELIVERED, stamps deliveredAt, records bottlesIn and customerRemark
2. Update the bottle ledgerIf bottles > 0, writes a BottleLedger RETURNED entry against the customer ("Collected by delivery executive")
3. AuditWrites a delivery.completed audit entry (with the bottle count) for the enterprise audit trail
4. Notify the customerFires notifyDelivered so the customer gets their delivery confirmation (with the bottle count)
5. Late-delivery checkThe portal runs the late-delivery monitor against the 7:00 AM promise; if late, it auto-sends an apology and shows the executive "⚠ Late by {N} min — apology sent"
ℹ️
Delivering twice is safe

The completion call is idempotent — if a stop is already DELIVERED, re-confirming simply returns the delivered status without creating a second bottle-ledger entry or a duplicate notification. A double tap on a flaky connection will not double-count bottles.

✅
Best practice — count bottles before you tap deliver

The bottle count entered here is what lands in the customer's bottle ledger and drives any deposit refund. Ask executives to physically count the empties, set the stepper, then confirm — correcting a bottle count after the stop is closed needs an office-side adjustment.

What "Submit report" does

Submitting sends POST /api/delivery/stop/{id} with action = issue. The backend creates a DeliveryIssue row tied to this delivery and this driver, with the mapped type, priority and comments, then writes a delivery.issue audit entry. The executive sees an "Issue reported to ops" toast. Reporting an issue does not mark the stop delivered — the stop stays open so it can be re-attempted or resolved.

Reference — data, persistence & the empty state

How the route is fetched, how progress is saved, and what a driver with no work sees.

Purpose Give support and admin staff the technical facts behind the field app so they can answer "why does the driver see X?".
Access Super Admin Admin (reference) Path Delivery Executive → Today → My Route

Where the route comes from

On load, the portal calls GET /api/delivery/my-route, which returns the driver header (name, employee ID, vehicle number, rating), the route (name and code), the date, an isFallbackDate flag, and the array of stops. It defaults to today; if today has no assignments it automatically returns the most recent day that does (hence the "(latest assigned day)" note in the hero). Each stop carries its contact, structured address, plan, quantity, expected/collected bottles, payment and the mapped status.

How progress is saved

Stop status and bottle counts are held in the phone's local storage (doodly-del-state) so the app is responsive even on a weak signal. In live mode the server is the source of truth — on every load the local state is re-seeded from the server's stop statuses and bottle counts, and each meaningful tap (status change, deliver, issue) is pushed to the backend. If a push fails, the app keeps the local note and shows a "Couldn't sync — will keep your local note" toast rather than losing the work.

The empty state

An executive with no assignments for the day (and none on any prior day) sees the hero sub-line "No route assigned yet" and, in the list area, the message "No deliveries assigned for today. New stops will appear here once your route is assigned." This is the correct, honest state — the field app never shows sample or demo stops on the live site.

Frequently asked questions

Can an executive see another driver's stops?

No. Both the route fetch and every stop update are scoped to the driver record linked to the signed-in user. A stop that is not on their route is rejected with a 403.

Does "Start route" notify customers?

Yes, indirectly. "Start route" moves every "Assigned" stop to "On the way", and the first move to on-the-way fires the "out for delivery" notification to that customer. It also begins the live GPS trail.

Where do the empty bottles collected go?

Into the customer's BottleLedger as a RETURNED entry (only when the count is above zero), written in the same transaction that marks the delivery complete. That ledger is what any bottle-deposit refund reads from.

Is the proof-of-delivery photo stored on the server?

In the current build the photo is a field-side confirmation aid shown as a thumbnail; the completion that is written to the backend records the delivered status, the bottle count and the note. Treat the photo as a courtesy capture, not a stored evidence file.
