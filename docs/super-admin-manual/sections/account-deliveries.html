Deliveries & Live Tracking

The customer's own record of every milk delivery — upcoming, delivered and skipped — plus a live, minute-by-minute tracking screen that follows the bottle from the farm to the doorstep.

ℹ️
Who sees this

These are two customer-facing account screens (/account/deliveries.html and /account/tracking.html). Every request is scoped strictly to the signed-in customer — the backend filters on subscription.userId OR order.userId, so one customer can never see another customer's deliveries. A Super Admin does not open these pages for a customer directly; the same delivery records are managed operationally under Admin → Operations → Delivery Mgmt. This section documents exactly what the customer experiences so support staff can guide them accurately.

The Deliveries table

The main block is the unified DataTable engine bound to the deliveries dataset. Each row is one delivery. The data is fetched from GET /api/deliveries (newest-first, up to 180 rows) and hydrated into the table by hydrateAccount().

Where the data comes from

GET /api/deliveries joins each Delivery to its subscription/order, and returns: id, date, status, slot, sequence, deliveredAt, bottlesOut, bottlesIn, bottleCount, customerRemark, driver name, order reference, plan name, an items summary and the formatted delivery address. The Deliveries table only surfaces six of these; the rest feed the Tracking and Calendar screens.

✅
Best Practice

When a customer disputes a delivery, ask them to open Deliveries, filter Status to the state in question (e.g. Skipped or Failed) and read out the Order reference (DOO-…). That reference maps one-to-one to the row a Super Admin opens under Delivery Mgmt, so both sides are looking at the exact same delivery.

Which delivery is shown

The tracking screen (wireTracking()) picks today's delivery from the customer's real rows; if there is none for today it falls back to the newest delivery on record. It then computes a stage index from the delivery status:

Stage indexTimeline node reachedBackend status(es)
0ScheduledSCHEDULED / ASSIGNED (anything not below)
1Packed at the farmPACKED, ACCEPTED
2Out for deliveryOUT_FOR_DELIVERY, ON_THE_WAY, REACHED
3DeliveredDELIVERED

If the status is FAILED or SKIPPED, the final node flips to "Not delivered" (a warning state) — "Skipped for this day" for a skip, or "We missed you — we'll make it right" for a failure.

The live map

The map card upgrades from a placeholder to a real map whenever a location is available. It calls GET /api/account/tracking immediately and then re-polls every 20 seconds until the delivery is finished or the customer leaves the page. The endpoint returns:

  • dest — the delivery address coordinates. If the saved address has no pin, the backend geocodes it once (keyless) and caches the result on the Address, so a destination marker appears even without a Google Maps key.
  • driver — the executive's live GPS (lat/lng/lastSeenAt), but only while the delivery is en route (status OUT_FOR_DELIVERY, ON_THE_WAY or REACHED). Otherwise the driver position is never exposed.
  • active, deliveryId, status, enRoute flags that drive the map state.

The map shows a "…is on the way" label with an "updated Ns/Nm ago" freshness stamp derived from lastSeenAt. Polling stops automatically once the delivery is delivered or no longer active (the drawn map stays on screen).

⚠️
Privacy — driver location is gated

The executive's live GPS is returned only for a delivery that is actively out for delivery to that specific customer. It is never shown for scheduled, packed, delivered, skipped or failed deliveries. Do not promise a customer "live driver tracking" before their bottle is on the way.

Related: the Live Order banner

That banner is mounted on the account dashboard home, not on the Deliveries page. From the Deliveries page the customer reaches live tracking via the Track live button.

✅
Best Practice

If a customer says the map is blank, first confirm their delivery status is actually Out for delivery / On the way / Reached. Before that stage there is intentionally no driver marker — only the destination pin (once the address has coordinates) will show.

Why does a delivery show a time in the Slot column but others show "—"?

The Slot column prefers the actual deliveredAt time once a delivery is completed. Before delivery it shows the scheduled slot; if neither exists it shows "—".

What is the difference between "Skipped" and "Failed"?

Skipped (grey) means the delivery was intentionally not made for that day — for example during a subscription pause. Failed (red) means an attempted delivery could not be completed; the tracking screen tells the customer the team has been notified.

Can a customer track someone else's delivery with a delivery ID?

No. Every delivery and tracking request is filtered to the authenticated user's own subscriptions and orders on the server, so a shared or guessed ID returns nothing for another account.

⛔
Caution — these are read-only customer views

The Deliveries and Tracking screens do not let the customer edit, cancel or reassign a delivery. Any operational change (reassigning an executive, marking delivered, resolving a failed delivery) must be done by staff under Admin → Operations → Delivery Mgmt. Never tell a customer they can change a delivery from these pages.
