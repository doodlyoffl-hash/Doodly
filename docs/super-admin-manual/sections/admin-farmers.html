Farmers

Register, monitor and pay the family-run buffalo farms that supply every bottle of DOODLY A2 milk.

The Farmers module is the master register of DOODLY's milk suppliers. Every row is a Farmer record — the farm's name, owner, village, collection route and, most importantly, its procurement rate in rupees per litre. The screen shows a live seven-card dashboard, a searchable and filterable list of farms, and two pop-up dialogs — Register farmer and Manage farmer — that together drive the whole supplier life-cycle: onboarding a farm, editing its details, viewing its collection and quality history, and settling its outstanding payments. Nothing on this page is invented on the client: it all reads and writes the production database through the /api/admin/farmers* endpoints, so what you see is the true state of the supply chain.

ℹ️
Note — one register, no duplicate tables

The Farmers module does not keep its own copy of collections, quality or payments. A farm's daily supply, average fat, quality result and money owed are all derived live from the shared Procurement and Quality Test records. That is why every farm here always agrees with the Procurement and Quality Testing screens — there is a single source of truth. The milk type is fixed platform-wide as A2 Buffalo.

The KPI strip

Seven live cards sit above the table, refreshed from GET /api/admin/farmers/stats every time the page loads or a change is saved. All figures are computed from the real Farmer, Procurement and Quality-Test tables — money values are stored in paise and printed as rounded rupees.

⚠️
Warning — the rate is entered in rupees but stored in paise

Type the rate in ordinary rupees (for example 62.00 for ₹62 per litre). The system multiplies by 100 and stores it as paise. Do not type 6200 thinking in paise — that would set the price to ₹6,200 per litre and inflate every future payment. Every procurement's payable amount is rate × litres, so an incorrect rate silently over- or under-pays the farm.

⚠️
Warning — Settle is a money action

Settle payments marks every unpaid collection for the farm as paid at once — it does not let you pick individual batches, and there is no "unsettle" button in this dialog. Only settle after the farmer has genuinely been paid (bank transfer / cash), because the paid stamp is what the Pending Pay KPI and the payment filter rely on.

✅
Best Practice — deactivate for a pause, soft-delete only when a farm truly leaves

If a farm stops supplying for a season, or is under review, clear the Active toggle rather than deleting it — this keeps its full history and payment record intact and is instantly reversible. Reserve Soft-delete for farms that have genuinely left the network. Because soft-delete only stamps a date, an admin can still restore the record later, and its past collections and quality history remain in the shared tables.

ℹ️
Note — the Procurement Manager role

The Procurement role is granted "manage" on farmers, which covers view, create, edit and export — but not delete. So a procurement manager can onboard farms, edit rates and settle payments, yet only a Super Admin or Admin can soft-delete or restore a farm. Every change is written to the audit log with the acting role.

ℹ️
Note — bulk endpoint

The platform exposes POST /api/admin/farmers/bulk for register-wide moves — activate, deactivate, assign route, delete and restore across up to 2,000 farms at once (delete/restore require farmers:delete). Every bulk run is audited. Day-to-day work on this screen is done per-farm through the Manage dialog.

⛔
Caution

Client-side role hiding is only for tidiness — the real security boundary is enforced on every API call. Rates, settlements and deletions affect real money owed to real farmers, so treat every save on this screen as a production action, not a test.

Where do a farm's litres, fat and money owed come from?

All of them are derived live from the shared Procurement and Quality Test records — the Farmers module keeps no separate copy. Supply is the 30-day average daily litres, Fat is the average across the farm's collections, and money owed is the sum of unpaid procurement amounts.

What does "Settle payments" actually do?

It marks every unpaid collection for that farm as paid, in one step, and tells you how many collections and how much money were settled. Do it only after the farmer has really been paid outside the system.

Can a Procurement Manager delete a farm?

No. Procurement can view, register, edit and settle, but soft-delete and restore require farmers:delete, which only Super Admin and Admin hold.

Is a soft-deleted farm gone for good?

No — soft-delete only stamps a deletedAt date and hides the farm from the default list. Its collections and quality history stay in the shared tables, and an admin can restore it (the bulk endpoint offers a restore action).

Why is the milk type always "A2 Buffalo"?

DOODLY sources a single milk type, so it is fixed platform-wide as a constant rather than a per-farm field.
