Late Delivery Monitoring

The screen that guards DOODLY's single biggest promise — fresh A2 buffalo milk at the doorstep before 7:00 AM every morning — by automatically catching every delivery that arrives late, apologising to the customer, scoring the delivery executive, escalating repeat offenders, and turning it all into reports and configurable rules.

ℹ️
What "Late Delivery Monitoring" does at DOODLY

Morning milk is only useful if it arrives on time. DOODLY commits to delivering before a promised clock time (7:00 AM by default, with an optional grace window). This screen watches every completed delivery: the moment one is delivered later than the promise, the system marks it Late, works out the delay in minutes, sends the customer a personalised apology (once), records the incident against the delivery executive, and — if that executive breaks the monthly threshold — escalates them to the Super Admin and Operations. You also get a live dashboard, an executive scoreboard, downloadable reports, and a Super-Admin configuration page for the promise time, apology wording and all the thresholds.

ℹ️
Live engine vs on-screen monitor

This screen has two layers working together. The on-screen monitor (DOODLY_LATE) draws every tab — Dashboard, Late Deliveries, Executive Performance, Reports and Configuration — and handles the detail pop-up, apology preview, filters and exports. The live detection engine runs on the DOODLY database: GET /api/admin/deliveries/late reads real Delivery records over the last 30 days, compares each against the SLA promise time stored in DeliveryConfig, and returns the true late count, average delay and per-executive figures. When the backend is reachable, the top five stat cards are overwritten with the live numbers and a green banner appears — "● Live — SLA late-detection on the DOODLY database …: promise 07:00, N late / N waiting · N% on-time." In production there is no fake history: the monitor shows only real completions fed in as deliveries are closed. This manual documents the live behaviour.

ℹ️
How the score is calculated

Score = on-time % − (deduct-per-complaint × complaints), clamped to the 0–100 range. In plain terms: start from the executive's on-time percentage, then subtract a small penalty for each complaint (2 points each by default). The band is then read off the thresholds on the Configuration tab — by default Excellent ≥ 95, Good ≥ 85, Needs Improvement ≥ 70, and anything lower is Critical. An executive is flagged when, over the current month, their late count reaches the "max late / month" limit or their on-time rate falls below the "min on-time rate". The live per-executive figures (total, late, on-time %, average delay) also come from the database via GET /api/admin/deliveries/late.

⚠️
Warning — the promise time changes what counts as late

Lowering the promise time or the grace window instantly makes more past and future deliveries "late" across the dashboard, scoreboard and reports; raising it makes fewer. Change it deliberately and tell the operations team, because it also feeds the live database detection that everyone else sees.

ℹ️
If the SLA save is refused

The rules always save on this screen, but the database SLA push is a separate step guarded by deliverySettings → edit. If your role lacks that right you'll see "Saved locally — your role can't edit SLA (403)": the on-screen monitor updates, but the live database detection keeps its previous promise time until a Super Admin saves it.

How detection, apology and escalation work

The automatic pipeline behind the screens.

Purpose Explain what happens the instant a delivery is completed, so you can trust the numbers and know why an apology or escalation fired.
Access Super Admin · Admin — reference only.
Workflow — one completed delivery
Delivery completedCompare actual vs promise + graceOn time? → recorded, no actionLate? → mark Late, compute delaySend apology (once)Log against executiveOver monthly threshold? → escalate
Figure — the automatic late-handling pipeline

When the driver app closes a delivery, the system compares the actual delivery time against the promise time plus grace:

  • On time (at or before the deadline) — recorded, nothing else happens.
  • Late — the incident is marked Late, the delay in minutes is calculated, a delivery.late_detected audit entry is written, and — if auto-apology is on — one apology is sent (a second automatic send is blocked so a customer is never apologised to twice for the same delivery).

On the live database the same idea runs over the last 30 days: a delivery marked DELIVERED after the SLA is Late; a delivery still open once the promise time has passed is Pending · late (a customer still waiting); a FAILED delivery reads Failed and a SKIPPED one reads Rescheduled.

Workflow — escalation (once per executive per month)
Late delivery loggedRecount executive's monthLate ≥ max/month OR on-time % < min rate?Raise escalationFlag on dashboard + scoreboardNotify Super Admin & Operations
Figure — when and how an executive is escalated

Escalation fires at most once per executive per month. On the live backend an escalation is recorded as a DeliveryIssue (type Delivery failed, priority High by default) via the late-action endpoint, while notify and resolve actions are written to the audit log (delivery.late.notify / delivery.late.resolve) — no new tables are added.

ℹ️
The customer sees their own version

Every customer has a small "Your delivery quality — last 30 days" card in their account showing their deliveries, on-time count, late count, average time and apologies received, measured against the same 7:00 AM promise from their own real delivery records. It is honest by design: a brand-new customer with no deliveries yet sees "Your delivery quality will appear here once your deliveries begin."

✅
Best Practice

Work the Late Deliveries log every morning: sort is already newest-and-worst first, so open the top rows, pick an accurate Reason (this is what powers the route and area trend panels), Mark reviewed, and only Resolve once the customer is genuinely looked after. Keep Auto-apology on so no late customer is ever left without a message, and re-check the "Executives requiring attention" panel weekly rather than waiting for the monthly escalation to fire.

Frequently asked questions

Q. Does changing the promise time affect deliveries that already happened?

A. Yes. "Late" is recalculated live against the current promise time and grace, so past deliveries can move between on-time and late when you change the setting. Change it deliberately.

Q. What exactly counts as "late"?

A. A delivery completed after the promise time plus the grace window. With the defaults (7:00 AM, 0 grace), a 7:01 AM completion is one minute late; set a 5-minute grace and the deadline becomes 7:05 AM.

Q. Who gets told when an executive is escalated?

A. The Super Admin and Operations. The executive is flagged on the dashboard and their scorecard, and on the live backend the escalation is filed as a High-priority delivery issue. It happens at most once per executive per month.

Q. Can I turn off automatic apologies?

A. Yes — set Auto-apology to Disabled on the Configuration tab. Late deliveries are still detected and logged; you then send apologies by hand from each delivery's detail pop-up.
