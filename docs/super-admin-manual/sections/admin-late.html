Late Delivery Monitoring

Protects the 7:00 AM morning-milk promise — it auto-detects deliveries that arrive after the promised time, sends the customer one apology, scores each Delivery Executive, escalates repeat offenders, and reports the trend across routes, areas and customers.

ℹ️
Note — what this module measures

DOODLY promises every morning milk delivery before 7:00 AM. A delivery is "late" when its actual delivery time is after the configured promise time plus any grace minutes. Delivered exactly at 7:00 AM counts as on-time. The module never changes an order or a payment — it only monitors timing, notifies customers, and holds executives accountable.

💡
How the data reaches this screen

In production the monitor reads real Delivery records from the last 30 days (up to 500) and compares each completed delivery's time against the SLA promise from Admin → Catalogue → Delivery Settings. Live driver completions also flow in the moment an executive marks a stop delivered, so a late delivery is flagged and the apology fires automatically — no one has to press a button.

⛔
Caution — the promise time is a company-wide commitment

Changing the Delivery promise time or Grace period instantly re-scores every executive and re-labels history as more or less late. It shares the same SLA value used across Delivery Settings and the late-detection engine, so change it only with an operational decision behind it — not to make a bad week look better.

💡
Tip — keep the score bands in order

Set the thresholds so Excellent ≥ Good ≥ Needs Improvement. If they overlap or invert, executives can land in the wrong band. The shipped 95 / 85 / 70 ladder is a sensible default for a fresh setup.

End-to-end workflow — from a late stop to a closed incident

Workflow — automatic late detection, apology and escalation
Executive marks stop delivered Actual time vs promise + grace Late? create delay record Send one apology (if auto-apology on) Log against the executive Audit the incident Over monthly threshold? escalate (once) Manager adds reason → review → resolve
Figure 8 — The detector runs on every completed delivery; managers finish the loop from the detail card.

Late detection is a simple, transparent rule: delay = actual delivery time − (promise time + grace). If the delay is greater than zero the delivery is late; delivered at or before the deadline is on-time. On the server, a delivery still un-delivered after the promise time shows as "Pending · late" so a stuck stop is visible before it is even completed; a FAILED delivery shows as "Failed" and a SKIPPED one as "Rescheduled".

Workflow — escalation guard
Monthly late count ≥ Max late / month OR monthly on-time % < Min on-time rate Already escalated this month? stop Raise escalation (once per executive per month) Flag on dashboard + alert Super Admin & Operations
Figure 9 — Escalation fires at most once per executive per month. On the server it also opens a HIGH-priority Delivery Issue.
ℹ️
Note — how the parts connect in production

The read screens are served by GET /api/admin/deliveries/late (needs deliveries:view), which reads real Delivery rows, applies the SLA from Delivery Settings, and returns the stats, the late list and the per-executive performance. The detail-card actions call POST /api/admin/deliveries/[id]/late-action (needs deliveries:edit) with notify, escalate or resolve. An escalate opens a HIGH-priority Delivery Issue for that driver; notify and resolve are written to the audit trail. No new database tables were added — escalations reuse Delivery Issues and notifications ride on the audit log.

Frequently asked questions

Is a delivery at exactly 7:00 AM counted as late?

No. Delivered at or before the promise time (plus any grace) is on-time. Only a delivery strictly after the deadline is late.

Does changing the grace period rewrite the past?

Yes. The delay for every delivery is recalculated live against the current promise time and grace, so history is re-labelled the moment you save. Adjust these settings deliberately.

Who gets told when an executive is escalated?

The dashboard shows an escalation banner and an on-screen alert flags the executive to the Super Admin and Operations. In production the escalation also opens a HIGH-priority Delivery Issue for that driver, which the operations team works from the delivery tools.

How is the executive score calculated?

It starts from the on-time rate for the selected period and subtracts the configured points for each complaint, clamped between 0 and 100. That score then maps to the Excellent / Good / Needs Improvement / Critical bands.

Can the apology message be customised per customer?

The wording is one shared template, but it automatically fills in each customer's name and the actual delivery time via the {{Customer Name}} and {{Actual Time}} placeholders, so every message reads personally.

✅
Best Practice — close the loop, don't just apologise

An apology soothes today; a recorded reason plus a Resolve builds the trend that fixes tomorrow. Make it routine: for every late delivery, pick a reason, confirm the apology, and resolve the incident once it is understood. The Reports tab then turns that discipline into the insight that protects the 7 AM promise.
