Notifications (campaigns)

Compose and blast WhatsApp, SMS, push and email campaigns to a live customer audience — every campaign is recorded, personalised and delivered straight from the DOODLY database.

ℹ️
What this screen is (and isn't)

This is a one-time marketing broadcast tool: pick an audience, pick a channel, type a message, and send. It is not the transactional notification system (order confirmed, out-for-delivery, delivered) — those fire automatically from order events and are not composed here. Both share the same delivery engine and the same customer consent rules, so a campaign only reaches people who have opted in to marketing on that channel.

💡
Personalisation variables

The message and title are personalised per recipient. These placeholders are supported: {{first_name}}, {{name}}, and {{referral_code}}. Any variable that has no value (or an unrecognised one) is replaced with a blank string, so the send never breaks. {{first_name}} falls back to "there" when the customer has no name on file.

⚠️
Send is immediate — there is no "save as draft" in this UI

The compose form always does create-and-send in one step. Once you click Send campaign the message goes out to everyone in the audience who has opted in. Check the live recipient count and re-read the message before sending — there is no undo, and deleting the campaign afterwards only removes it from history, it does not recall messages already delivered.

ℹ️
Consent gating — who actually gets the message

The audience count is who matches the segment; the delivered count is who also consented. At send time each recipient is filtered by their CustomerPreference: they must have the global marketing opt-in on and the per-channel opt-in for the chosen channel. Defaults when a customer has never set preferences: Email on, WhatsApp on, Push on, SMS off. So an SMS campaign will typically reach fewer people than the segment size unless customers have explicitly enabled SMS.

ℹ️
How delivery finishes for large audiences

Push is in-app only, so those rows are marked delivered the instant they are created. Email, SMS and WhatsApp rows are queued and dispatched inline within a short time budget (~9 seconds) so a big audience never times out the request. Anything left over stays queued and is finished by the daily drain job (or a manual retry). That is why a fresh campaign can briefly show status Sending before it settles to Sent.

⛔
Delete removes the record, not the messages

Deleting a campaign only takes it out of the history list. Messages already delivered to customers (in-app, email, SMS or WhatsApp) are not recalled — a broadcast cannot be un-sent. Treat delete as "tidy up the log", never as "cancel the campaign".

⚠️
Fallback-safe by design

If a channel has no provider key, its sends are marked skipped (never an error) and the campaign quietly degrades to in-app delivery. That is why the live banner reminds you: "External channels also need provider credentials." If an SMS or WhatsApp campaign shows a low delivered count, first confirm the provider is configured — not just that customers opted in.

✅
Best Practice

Send yourself (or a test account that opted into all channels) a copy first by narrowing to a small segment, and always fill the optional Campaign name — a clear name like "Diwali 15% off · Active subscribers" makes the history table and audit log readable months later. Keep messages short, personalise with {{first_name}}, and never blast SMS as a first channel since it is off by default and DLT-template-restricted.

Can I schedule a campaign for later?

No. This screen only does create-and-send immediately. There is no scheduler or "save as draft" in the compose form — send it when you are ready.

Can I edit a campaign after sending?

No. A sent campaign is a delivery record; you can delete it from history but you cannot change or resend the same record. Compose a new campaign instead.

Do transactional messages (order confirmed, out for delivery) come from here?

No. Those fire automatically from order and delivery events through the same delivery engine and consent rules, but they are not composed on this screen and do not appear in the campaign history.

Why did a customer not receive my Push campaign externally?

Push is in-app only — it appears in the customer's DOODLY notification inbox, it is never sent to a phone's OS push service. Use Email, SMS or WhatsApp for messages that must reach people outside the app.
