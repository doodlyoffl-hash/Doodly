Invoices

The customer's own record of every billed order — a searchable, filterable list of GST tax invoices that each open into a full printable document and download as a receipt.

ℹ️
Where this lives

This is a Customer-surface page reached at /account/invoices.html. A logged-in customer sees their own invoices; a Super Admin or Admin viewing the customer account (for support) sees the same screen. The page is built from two real pieces of code: the invoices data table (registered in blocks.js, driven by the shared DataTable engine in datatable.js) and the full tax-invoice document that opens on View (built by invoice.js, DOODLY_INVOICE.mountB2C).

Where the invoice records come from

The table reads its rows from DOODLY_DATA.invoices. Each record carries exactly these fields: id, date, amount (a number), gst (a label such as "incl. GST") and status (a colour + label pair, e.g. ["green","Paid"]). Per the page's own copy, invoices are generated automatically after each paid order; the customer never creates one by hand on this screen. The customer Dashboard reuses these same records for its "Latest Invoice" and "Pending Payments" cards (an unpaid invoice is any record whose status label is not "Paid").

Table controls (DataTable engine)

The list is powered by the shared DataTable engine. The controls below are what the customer actually gets on this table.

ControlBehaviour on this table
SearchInstant, type-ahead box (placeholder "Search invoices…"). Matches across all visible cell text — invoice number, date, amount, tax label and status. Debounced (~180 ms) and resets to page 1 on each keystroke.
FiltersA Filters button opens a side panel with auto-detected facet groups. For invoices the engine detects a Status facet and lists a checkbox for each distinct status found in the data (for example "Paid", and "Pending"/"Overdue" once such invoices exist). The Status group only appears when the account has two or more distinct statuses; with a single status there is nothing to filter by.
Date rangeBecause the Date column holds real dates, the engine offers date-range presets plus a custom from/to range so the customer can narrow to a month, quarter or year.
SortClick any column header to sort; click again to reverse; multiple columns can be stacked. A ▲/▼ indicator marks the sorted column. Sorting is by the visible text of the cell.
Filter chipsEvery active search term, status filter, date range and sort shows as a removable chip above the table; clicking the ✕ on a chip clears just that condition.
Saved views & favoritesThe customer's current search / filters / sort / page-size is remembered per user and per table (in local storage) so the view is restored on the next visit.
ExportAn Export ▾ menu offers CSV (.csv), Excel (.xls) and PDF of the currently filtered rows. On this customer page export is available (no admin module permission gates it).
PaginationRows are paged, 25 per page by default, with a pager at the foot of the table.
Empty stateWhen the account genuinely has no invoices, a designed empty state appears — a receipt icon, "No invoices yet", the line "Invoices are generated automatically after each paid order — you'll be able to view and download them here", and a Browse products button. When a filter simply matches nothing, a lighter "No results found — try adjusting your search or filters" message with a Clear all button is shown instead.
⚠️
The Status filter is data-driven, not a fixed toggle

The Status facet is generated from whatever statuses are actually present. Do not promise a customer an "All / Paid" switch: if every invoice on the account is Paid, the engine has nothing to filter and the Status group is hidden. The full list (the "All" case) is simply the table with no filter applied.

Best Practice

When helping a customer find one invoice, use the Date range plus the Search box together (e.g. type the amount or the last four digits of the invoice number) rather than scrolling. Then use the row Download button for a quick receipt, and View only when the customer needs the full itemised, printable document.

Caution — the premium document is template-only on the live site

On production, the full B2C invoice document deliberately shows a clean empty state — "No invoice to display · Your tax invoice will appear here once your billing has been generated" — because these premium documents are not yet wired to live billing and must never display fabricated figures. The rich itemised document above renders with data only when demo mode is allowed. The customer's Invoices list (Screen 1) and its per-row Download CSV are the reliable path today; treat the on-screen document as a template until live billing is connected.

Tip

Set the Number format and Terms & conditions once, at go-live, and leave them. Changing the number format later can make new invoice numbers look inconsistent next to older ones. Use the Watermark field ("DRAFT") only while testing, and clear it before real invoices go out.

Troubleshooting & FAQ

Common questions when helping a customer with invoices.

SymptomLikely causeFix
The list shows "No invoices yet" for a paying customer.No invoice records have been generated for the account yet (invoices are raised automatically after a paid order), or you are on a fresh session with no data loaded.Confirm the customer has at least one paid order. Invoices appear only after payment settles; a pending order will not have an invoice.

Is this the same as Business Invoices?

No. This customer page shows personal (B2C) tax invoices for a household account. Commercial partners have a separate Business Partner Statement under Admin → Commerce → Business Invoices, with credit limits, outstanding trackers and supply analytics.

Can a customer edit or delete an invoice here?

No. The list is read-only for the customer — they can only search, filter, sort, open (View) and download. Invoices are generated by the system after payment; only a Super Admin can change how they are branded and numbered, via Invoice Settings.

Where do the seller name, GSTIN and address on the downloaded receipt come from?

From the brand's company profile (legal name, GSTIN, registered address). They are stamped onto the CSV automatically at download time, so every receipt carries the correct seller details.
