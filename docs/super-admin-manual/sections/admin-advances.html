Salary Advances

Request, approve and recover staff salary advances — a small loan against future pay that DOODLY automatically claws back, installment by installment, from the employee's next payslips.

ℹ️
What a "salary advance" is at DOODLY

A salary advance is money paid to a team member ahead of payday — for an emergency, a festival, a medical bill. HR raises the advance, a decision-maker approves it, and payroll then recovers it in equal installments (up to 24 salary cycles) by deducting from each month's net pay until the full amount is repaid. This screen is the control room for that whole lifecycle: raise, approve/reject, and watch the outstanding balance shrink. It shares one backend with Salary Structure and Payroll — every rupee is stored in paise (₹1 = 100 paise) so nothing is ever lost to rounding.

ℹ️
How the installment is worked out

You enter the total amount and the number of installments; DOODLY does the maths. Per-installment = ceil(amount ÷ installments), so a ₹15,000 advance over 6 cycles becomes 6 × ₹2,500. The final installment is automatically trimmed so the employee never repays more than they borrowed — recovery stops the moment Remaining hits ₹0. Recovery is always taken from salary (the recovery method defaults to SALARY; the form does not offer a lump-sum option).

⚠️
Submitting does not pay any money out

Submit advance only creates a Pending record and writes an audit entry (advance.create). No cash moves and no recovery begins until someone approves it. The advance is auto-numbered ADV-0001, ADV-0002… and its Remaining balance is set to the full amount.

⚠️
A decision can only be made once

The server checks the advance is still Pending before acting. If it was already approved or rejected (for example by a colleague a moment earlier), the request is refused with "This advance is already decided." Reload the page to see the current state. There is no "undo" and no "edit" on a decided advance — approve or reject is final.

How advances are recovered (via Payroll)

Advances are never collected here — Payroll deducts them automatically from each payslip.

ℹ️
Recovery is idempotent and oldest-first

Recovery is applied once per payslip — re-marking or reloading never double-deducts, because DOODLY checks for existing recovery ledger rows before applying. When an employee has more than one live advance, the current cycle's recovery is applied to their oldest advance first (by creation date) until this cycle's planned amount is used up.

💡
Tip — to actually reduce a balance, pay the payslip

Approving an advance sets up the plan, but the Remaining figure on this screen only moves when the matching payslip is marked Paid in Admin → Human Resources → Payroll. If a balance "isn't going down", check whether that month's payslip has actually been paid.

✅
Best Practice

Clear the Pending queue daily — filter by status "Pending" and decide each request while the reason is fresh, because the employee is waiting and nothing recovers until you approve. Always type a reason when you reject: it is stored on the advance and sent to the employee, so a blank rejection just creates a follow-up question. Set the installments to something the employee's take-home can absorb — a large advance recovered in one cycle can wipe out a payslip; spreading it over several months keeps their salary livable while still clearing the balance. Reconcile the Outstanding KPI against your books at month-end; it is the single source of truth for money the company is still owed.

Frequently asked questions

Q. Who can raise and approve advances?

A. Super Admin, Admin and Accountant. Viewing needs the payroll permission at "view"; raising, approving and rejecting need it at "edit". The client hides the buttons for other roles, but the real check is on the server — a role without payroll access is refused with a 403.

Q. Does approving an advance immediately deduct money?

A. No. Approving only starts the recovery plan and notifies the employee. The actual deduction happens later, cycle by cycle, when each of the employee's payslips is marked Paid in Payroll.

Q. Can I edit or cancel an advance after it's approved?

A. There is no edit or cancel action. An advance moves PENDING → APPROVED → COMPLETED (as it is recovered) or PENDING → REJECTED, and a decision can't be reversed. If a mistake was made, raise a correcting entry and note it in the reason field.

Q. What happens if an employee has several advances at once?

A. Each payslip plans a combined "Advance recovery" and, at Mark-paid, pays them off oldest first until this cycle's planned amount is used up. Each advance tracks its own Recovered and Remaining independently and completes on its own.

Q. What's in the CSV export?

A. The rows currently loaded (respecting your status filter and search), with Advance code, Employee, Emp Code, Department, Amount, Installments, Recovered, Remaining and Status — amounts in rupees — saved as doodly-advances.csv.

Q. What is the maximum advance and how many installments are allowed?

A. Up to ₹10,00,000 per advance, recovered over 1 to 24 salary cycles. Entering "1" installment recovers the whole amount from the next payslip.
