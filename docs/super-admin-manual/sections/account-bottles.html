Bottle Tracking

A read-only, per-customer ledger of every reusable glass bottle DOODLY has issued, collected back, or lost — plus the refundable deposit held against them.

ℹ️
Where this screen sits

Bottle Tracking is a customer self-service page at /account/bottles.html. It shows the signed-in customer only their own ledger. A Super Admin normally reviews this screen while helping a customer over support, or reconciles the same numbers from the operations side under Admin → Catalogue → Bottle Inventory (the warehouse fleet) and the driver's Bottle Collection task list. This page never edits the ledger — entries are written automatically by delivery, checkout and refund flows (see the Workflow below).

ℹ️
Two different counting methods — on purpose

Total issued and Returned count the number of ledger entries of that kind, while Pending uses the sum of bottle quantities (ISSUED − RETURNED − LOST). In everyday use each delivery issues one bottle (qty 1), so the numbers line up. If you ever see "Total issued" and "Pending" disagree in a way maths can't explain, check for a ledger row with qty > 1 or a LOST event — those are the two cases where the entry-count and the quantity-sum diverge.

How a ledger entry is born

The customer never writes to this ledger — it is append-only and populated automatically by operational events. Understanding those triggers is how a Super Admin explains any number on this page.

Best Practice

When a customer disputes their pending-bottle count, don't adjust anything from this screen — it is read-only by design. Instead trace the underlying delivery: an over-count almost always means an ISSUED row exists without its matching RETURNED row because a driver forgot to tick "collected empty" at the stop. Fix it at the source (the driver's delivery record), and this ledger and every KPI recompute themselves.

💡
Tip — the deposit is separate from the bottle count

DEPOSIT_CHARGED / DEPOSIT_REFUNDED events move money (₹), not the bottle balance. That is why the "Deposit held" card links to the Wallet, while the "Pending" card links to Bottle Return. A refund shows here as a green "Refund" row and simultaneously as a credit in the customer's wallet.

⚠️
Warning — the API returns the 120 most recent rows

GET /api/bottles returns at most the 120 latest ledger entries for the table. The Pending and Deposit held figures are computed server-side from all-time grouped totals, so they stay correct even beyond 120 rows — but "Total issued" and "Returned" cards (which count visible rows) could under-count for an extremely long-tenured customer. Trust the Pending and Deposit numbers as the authoritative balances.

Frequently asked questions

Q. Can a Super Admin edit or delete a bottle ledger entry from this page?

No. The ledger is append-only and this screen is strictly read-only. Corrections are made by writing a new, opposite event (for example a RETURNED to offset an over-issued bottle) through the delivery/operations flow — never by altering history.

Q. Why does "Pending" link to Bottle Return but "Deposit held" links to the Wallet?

Because they represent two different things: Pending is a bottle count the customer clears by handing empties back (Bottle Return), while Deposit held is money that is refunded into the Wallet once the glass is returned.

Q. Where does the "Request collection" button actually take the customer?

To the public /bottle-return.html page that explains the reusable-glass loop. Pickup itself happens on the next delivery — the button starts the conversation; the driver's collection creates the RETURNED ledger row.

Q. Is this the same as the admin "Bottle Inventory" screen?

No. This customer page is the per-customer deposit-and-empties ledger. Admin → Catalogue → Bottle Inventory tracks the physical fleet of glass bottles by capacity and lifecycle stage across the whole business. They reconcile against each other but are separate views.
