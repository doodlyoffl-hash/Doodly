Where this lives

This is a Customer-facing screen inside the customer account area at Admin → Customer Module → Support (/account/support.html). Every ticket a customer raises here lands in the staff desk at Admin → System → Support Tickets (/admin/support.html). A Super Admin uses this page to understand exactly what a customer sees and does; the staff-side triage, assignment and reply tools are documented in the Support Tickets module.

The list you see is live, not the demo table

The page recipe includes a placeholder "tickets" data table, but on load the account script hides it and injects the real "Your tickets" panel above it, fed by the backend. So a customer never sees stale sample rows — only their own tickets.

Form — "Raise a ticket"

Three fields, laid out in two columns (the Message box spans the full width). The form submits to the backend as a new ticket; identity (name, email, phone) is taken from the signed-in account and is never typed here.

ℹ️
Field names are auto-derived

The form builder turns each label into a lowercase machine name (Subject → subject, Category → category, Message → message). The submit handler reads those exact names, so renaming a label in the recipe would also rename the field the backend receives.

ℹ️
What the customer can and cannot see

The backend deliberately returns only safe fields to the customer: ticket number, subject, category, status & status label, priority & priority label, and the created / updated / resolved dates. Internal details — the assignee, SLA due date, internal notes, resolution text and tags — are never sent to the browser.

Ticket lifecycle

Workflow — a customer ticket from raise to close
Customer submits form Ticket created — Open, TK-#### Staff assign → Assigned Staff work → In Progress Waiting for Customer (if info needed) Resolved Closed
Figure — the six ticket statuses. The customer only ever performs the first step; the status badge on this page updates as staff move the ticket forward.

When the form is submitted, the system:

  1. Generates the next ticket number in the form TK-<n> (the counter starts around TK-2042 and steps forward, skipping any number already used).
  2. Creates the ticket with status Open and default priority Medium (the customer form does not set priority).
  3. Stamps a target response time behind the scenes based on priority — 4 h Urgent, 8 h High, 24 h Medium, 48 h Low. This SLA is staff-only and is not shown to the customer.
  4. Attaches the customer's message as the first entry on the ticket thread, plus a system note recording that the ticket was created.
  5. Fills the customer's name, email and phone from their signed-in account — these are trusted from the session, never from the form.
✅
Best Practice

Coach customers (and support staff onboarding on this page) to pick the most specific Category and write a clear one-line Subject. Good categorisation drives the desk's routing and reporting; a vague "Other / help" ticket is slower to triage. For anything time-critical (missed delivery, payment failure), the Start chat WhatsApp card gets a faster human response than a ticket.

Q · Can a customer edit or reply to a ticket from this page?

A. Not from here. This screen is for raising a ticket and tracking its status. All replies, status changes and resolution notes are handled by staff in the Support Tickets desk; the customer sees the outcome as an updated status badge.

Q · Where does the ticket number come from, and is it unique?

A. The system assigns a sequential TK-#### number at creation and checks for collisions before saving, so each ticket has its own unique number that both the customer and staff use to refer to it.

Q · Does raising a ticket set a priority?

A. Every customer ticket starts at Medium priority. Support staff can raise or lower priority (Low / Medium / High / Urgent) on the desk side; the customer sees the current priority label but cannot set it.

Q · What's the difference between "Live chat" here and the on-screen assistant?

A. The Live chat card opens WhatsApp to reach a human on the DOODLY support line. That is separate from any in-app assistant widget; use the card when the customer wants a real person quickly, and a ticket when they want a written, tracked record of the issue.
