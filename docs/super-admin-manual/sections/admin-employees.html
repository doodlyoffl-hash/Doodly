Employees

The Employee Master — the single place to add staff, record their employment and personal details, keep their identity and bank documents safely, and manage their working status across their whole time at DOODLY.

The Employees page is the master record for every person who works at DOODLY — delivery executives, procurement and production staff, support agents, accountants, managers and admins. In DOODLY an employee is not a separate thing from a login account: each employee record is joined one-to-one to a staff user account. When you add an employee here, DOODLY quietly creates their sign-in account too (with the login role you pick), and when you mark them Resigned, Terminated or remove them, that same account is switched off. This means you manage the whole person — who they are, what they do, and whether they can sign in — from one screen.

Every employee gets an auto-generated employee code in the form EMP-0001, EMP-0002 and so on (four digits, counted up and checked so two people never share a code). This code, not the name, is the stable handle used across Attendance, Leave, Salary Advances and Payroll.

The page is built from three parts that we document in turn: a live statistics strip at the top, the employee directory table with its search and filters, and the Add / Edit employee form that opens as a pop-up. We then cover the employee detail view (opened by the View button on each row) and the photo & documents area inside the form.

ℹ️
Where the data lives

This page is fully backend-driven — it reads and writes real records through /api/admin/employees (Postgres). There is no demo data. An employee record is an EmployeeProfile row joined 1:1 to a User account: HR fields (department, identity, bank, documents) live on the profile; the name, email, phone, login role and account status live on the user. Every create, edit, status change, delete and document upload is written to the Audit Log.

⚠️
Who can open this page

Employees is an HR module. Super Admin and Admin have full access (view, create, edit, delete). Accountant has view-only access and sees sensitive identity numbers masked. All other roles (Operations, Procurement, Inventory, Quality, Marketing, Support, Delivery Executive, Customer) have no access to this page at all. Permission is enforced on the server for every request — the buttons you see are only the UX half of the same rule.

ℹ️
Status is tied to the login account

Setting an employee to Resigned or Terminated automatically disables their staff sign-in account, so they can no longer log in. Setting them back to Active re-enables the account. On Leave keeps the account enabled — leave is about attendance, not access.

⚠️
"Login role" grants real power

The Login role decides what the person can do once they sign in — pick the lowest role that lets them do their job. A Delivery Executive should not be given Admin. Choosing Admin or Super Admin here creates a high-privilege account, so use those sparingly and only for people who genuinely need them.

⛔
Caution — do not overwrite masked numbers

When you edit an employee and a number shows as masked ("•••• 1234"), the form deliberately clears that box so you do not accidentally save the mask over the real value. Leaving it blank keeps the stored number safe. Only type into these boxes if you actually want to replace the number. If you have view-only access you cannot type a real value in anyway.

ℹ️
Why photos & documents wait until after the first save

Files are stored against the employee's ID, which only exists once the record is created. So when you first add someone, the Photo & documents area says "Save the employee first, then reopen this record to add a photo and documents." Save, click View, then Edit, and the upload controls appear.

⚠️
Remove is a soft-delete — but there is no Restore button here

"Remove" does not erase anything: it hides the record, marks it Terminated, and stamps a deletion time so history (attendance, payslips, audit) stays intact. However, the Employees page does not offer an in-page "Restore". If you removed the wrong person, the record still exists in the database — recovering it needs a Super Admin / engineering step, not a button on this screen. So confirm carefully before removing.

Masking, precisely

Only the four most sensitive numbers are masked for view-only roles: Aadhaar, PAN, Driving licence and Bank account (shown as "•••• " plus the last four digits). IFSC, Bank name and UPI are shown in full to any viewer. Uploaded document files are only openable by roles that can edit employees.

✅
Best Practice

Add every real team member here rather than only in User Management — this way their department, joining date, documents and payroll all hang off one record and their EMP-#### code flows into Attendance, Leave, Advances and Payroll. Give each person the lowest login role that fits their job. Set the reporting manager so the org chart and leave approvals make sense. Keep Aadhaar/PAN/bank details accurate for payroll, and upload the matching proof documents. When someone goes on extended absence use Mark on leave (keeps their login); when they leave the company set Resigned/Terminated or Remove to cut access immediately — and double-check before removing, since there is no one-click restore on this page.

Frequently asked questions

Does adding an employee also create their login account?

Yes. Each employee is joined one-to-one to a staff user account. When you click Add employee, DOODLY creates the backing account with the Login role you chose and sets it Active. That is why the phone and email must be unique across all accounts.

How is the employee code decided?

Automatically. DOODLY takes the current employee count, forms the next EMP-#### (four digits, zero-padded), and checks it isn't already taken before assigning it. You never type it — it appears once the record is saved, e.g. EMP-0007.

Why can the Accountant see employees but not the full Aadhaar or bank number?

The Accountant role has view-only access so it can reference the roster for payroll, but the four most sensitive numbers (Aadhaar, PAN, driving licence, bank account) are masked to the last four digits. Full numbers and the ability to edit are limited to Admin and Super Admin.

What's the difference between "Mark on leave" and "Resigned/Terminated"?

Mark on leave is for a temporary absence — the person stays employed and their login keeps working. Resigned/Terminated means they have left; DOODLY disables their sign-in account automatically. Use On Leave for people coming back, and Resigned/Terminated for people who aren't.

If I "Remove" someone by mistake, is their data gone?

No — Remove is a soft-delete. The record is hidden, marked Terminated and time-stamped, and their login is disabled, but nothing (attendance, payslips, audit) is erased. There is no one-click restore on this page, so recovering the record needs a Super Admin / backend step — which is exactly why the button asks you to confirm first.

Where do I set someone's salary or mark their attendance?

The employee's Salary structure is reachable from the detail view (needs Payroll rights). Day-to-day attendance, leave, salary advances and payroll runs each have their own screens under Admin → Human Resources, all keyed to the same employee code.
