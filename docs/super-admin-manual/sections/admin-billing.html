Subscription Billing (AutoPay)

One control room for every recurring-payment mandate — see who has hands-free renewals, which renewals are coming up, which have failed, and step in to retry, pause, resume or cancel any customer's AutoPay.

ℹ️
What "AutoPay" means at DOODLY

AutoPay is a Razorpay recurring e-mandate attached to a customer's milk subscription. Once the customer authorises it (their bank / UPI app approves a standing instruction), DOODLY charges the renewal amount automatically before the plan expires — so the morning delivery never stops. AutoPay is subscription-only and strictly opt-in: a mandate is only ever created when a customer ticks "Enable AutoPay" at checkout. This admin screen does not create mandates — it monitors and manages the ones that already exist.

Live vs demo

When the backend is reachable the screen loads real mandates from GET /api/admin/autopay and shows a green banner: "● Live — N AutoPay mandate(s) from the DOODLY database" with the API base. If the backend is offline (and demo mode is allowed) it shows a red "⚠ Backend offline — showing demo AutoPay data" banner over a sample table. In production with no demo mode the list is driven purely by real mandates and simply shows an empty state when there are none. This manual documents the live behaviour.

ℹ️
"Failed" vs "Suspended"

Failed renewals (30d) counts individual failed charges in the payment history — a mandate can fail once, be retried by Razorpay, and still recover. A mandate only moves to Suspended after Razorpay exhausts its automatic retries. Suspended and Retry-pending mandates are the ones that need a human — filter the table by Suspended or Retry to find them.

Row actions — what each button does

Only the buttons that make sense for a mandate's current status are shown. Every action posts to POST /api/admin/autopay with the mandate's gateway id, calls the corresponding Razorpay control, updates the mandate, writes an audit record, then reloads the table. While an action is in flight the row's buttons are disabled, and on success a toast confirms ("AutoPay retried ✓", etc.).

ℹ️
Why "Retry" and "Resume" behave the same

Under the hood both Retry and Resume tell Razorpay to resume the subscription and flip the mandate back to Active with the attempt counter cleared. "Retry" is simply the label used when a mandate is in a failed state (Suspended / Retry), and "Resume" when it was deliberately paused (Inactive) — the customer or gateway then re-attempts the outstanding cycle. "Suspend" is the mirror of "Resume": it pauses an Active mandate.

⚠️
Warning — permission is enforced on the server

The buttons you see are filtered by your role in the browser, but the real security check is on the backend: GET /api/admin/autopay requires billing → view and POST requires billing → edit. A role without billing access gets a 403 and the page shows "Your role can't view subscription billing." Do not rely on hidden buttons alone — the server is the boundary.

⚠️
Warning — you cannot change a customer's card/UPI from here

Razorpay mandates are bound to the exact card or UPI the customer authorised; they cannot be swapped silently. To move a customer to a new payment method, they must cancel AutoPay and re-enable it at their next checkout, where their bank re-authorises a fresh e-mandate. There is no "change method" action on the admin table.

⛔
Caution — Cancel is a hard stop

Cancel tells Razorpay to cancel the recurring subscription and sets the mandate to CANCELLED and the subscription's autoRenew to off. There is no "un-cancel" — the customer must enable AutoPay again at a future checkout, which requires a brand-new bank authorisation. If the goal is only to stop charging for a while, use Suspend (reversible with Resume) instead of Cancel.

ℹ️
Every action is audited

Each admin action writes an audit record — admin.autopay.retry / admin.autopay.resume / admin.autopay.suspend / admin.autopay.cancel — tagged with your role and the mandate id, on top of the service-level audit (autopay.resume, autopay.pause, autopay.cancel). A Super Admin can always trace who touched a mandate and when in the Admin → System → Audit Logs trail.

✅
Best Practice

Work the failures first. Each morning, filter the table by Suspended and then Retry, and clear that queue: call or message the customer to check their card/UPI, then hit Retry once they confirm. Keep an eye on Failed renewals (30d) as a trend — a rising number usually means an expired-card wave, not a one-off. Reserve Cancel for genuine churn; for a customer who simply wants a break, use Suspend so their plan and mandate survive.

Frequently asked questions

Q. Can I set up AutoPay for a customer from this screen?

A. No. A mandate can only be created when the customer opts in at checkout, because their bank must authorise the recurring e-mandate. This screen manages existing mandates — it has no "create" or "enable" action.

Q. What is the difference between Suspend and Cancel?

A. Suspend pauses automatic charging but keeps the mandate — you can Resume it later with no re-authorisation. Cancel terminates the mandate permanently and turns the subscription's auto-renew off; re-enabling requires a fresh authorisation at a future checkout.

Q. Does cancelling AutoPay cancel the customer's subscription?

A. No. Cancelling the mandate only stops automatic renewals — it sets the subscription's autoRenew to off. The subscription itself continues for its remaining term; the customer can renew manually or re-enable AutoPay at checkout.

Q. Why does a mandate show "Suspended" instead of just being cancelled after a failed payment?

A. By design DOODLY never silently cancels. When Razorpay's retries are exhausted the mandate is set to Suspended and the customer is notified that deliveries are paused (not cancelled) so they can fix their payment method and resume — protecting both the customer's service and DOODLY's recurring revenue.

Q. What does the Export CSV include?

A. The rows currently on screen (respecting your active search and status filter), with Customer, Phone, Email, Plan, Amount in rupees, Next renewal date, Attempts, Status and the gateway Mandate id — saved as autopay-mandates-YYYY-MM-DD.csv.
