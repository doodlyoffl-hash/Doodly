Pure Rewards (Loyalty)

Configure every earning rate, redemption ratio, tier threshold, expiry window and bonus campaign of the DOODLY Pure Rewards programme, watch programme-wide KPIs, and manually grant or deduct a member's points — with every change written to the audit trail.

ℹ️
One page, three working areas

The whole module lives at one screen (/admin/loyalty.html, block type loyaltyAdmin, mount #loyaltyAdminMount). It loads two calls in parallel — GET /api/admin/loyalty/config and GET /api/admin/loyalty/members — then paints a KPI strip, a Programme-settings form, and a Members table. Each is documented below as its own screen.

✅
The settings row is the single source of truth

Every rule lives in one database row (LoyaltyConfig, id singleton). Nothing about earning, redeeming, tiers or expiry is hard-coded — what you save here is exactly what customers experience on their Rewards page and what every automatic earn hook (order paid, subscription, referral, bottle return, birthday, puzzle, review…) reads at run time.

⚠️
The master switch pauses the entire programme

The Programme ON/OFF toggle writes enabled. When OFF, every automatic earn hook returns early ("disabled"), redemption is refused, and the customer Rewards page shows "Rewards are taking a short break — your points are safe." Existing balances are never touched; the programme simply stops accruing and paying out until you switch it back on.

💡
Set a rate to 0 to switch that earn off

Any earn hook that resolves to 0 points is skipped ("zero") — no ledger row is written. So you don't need code to disable, say, birthday points; just set the field to 0.

⛔
Changing the ratio does not restate points already earned

The redemption ratio is read at the moment of redemption. If you raise "Points per ₹1" from 10 to 20, existing balances instantly become worth half as much in wallet credit. Announce ratio changes; they affect every member's live balance value even though the point counts don't change.

ℹ️
A campaign needs both fields

A multiplier above 1 does nothing on its own — the end date must be a future date for the boost to count. When live, a "Bonus campaign live — earn X% extra points" banner appears on the customer Rewards page. When the date passes, earning quietly returns to the base rates.

Tiers

The tier a customer sits in is derived from their lifetime points earned — redeeming or expiring points never demotes them. Each tier card has three inputs. You must keep at least one tier (1–10 allowed); the server sorts them by minimum points ascending and ignores any card whose name is blank.

ℹ️
Available vs. Lifetime earned

Available is what the member can redeem now (loyaltyPoints). Lifetime earned only ever goes up and decides the tier; redeeming or expiry lowers the Available balance but never the lifetime figure, so a customer keeps their tier even after spending their points.

⛔
A deduction is not a redemption — it does not credit the wallet

Deducting points simply removes them from the balance (oldest lots first) and records an ADJUST ledger row. No money moves. Use a deduction only to correct wrongly-granted points; never as a way to "pay" a customer. To convert points to money, the customer redeems on their Rewards page.

✅
Always write a specific reason

The reason is the audit trail. Write "Restored 200 pts lost to expiry bug, ticket #4821" rather than "fix". Every adjustment is logged with your role, the member, the signed amount and this reason — a vague reason makes a later review impossible.

ℹ️
Earning is idempotent and event-anchored

Each earn carries a deterministic reference (for example earn:order:<id>, earn:reg:<uid>, earn:birthday:<uid>:<year>). That reference is unique, so a webhook retry or a double event awards the points at most once. This is why order points are safe to fire from both the payment callback and the webhook.

ℹ️
Redemption is the only place money is created

Redeeming credits the customer's DOODLY Wallet through a normal wallet transaction (kind loyalty) inside one atomic, serializable transaction. Points balances and wallet balances therefore never drift out of sync, and wallet history shows the redemption like any other credit.

Frequently asked questions

Do customers have to sign up for Pure Rewards?

No. Every registered DOODLY customer is enrolled automatically. There is no separate opt-in; they simply start earning.

If I lower an earning rate, do customers lose points they already earned?

No. Rate changes apply only to future earns. Points already awarded keep their value and their original 12-month (or whatever expiryDays was at award time) expiry.

Can a customer's tier go down when they redeem?

No. Tiers are based on lifetime points earned, which never decreases. Redeeming and expiry reduce the available balance but never the tier.

Who can adjust a member's points, and is it tracked?

Only roles with loyalty:edit (Super Admin, Admin, Marketing by default). Every adjustment is written to the audit log as loyalty.adjust with the member, the signed amount and the reason you type.

What happens to points when an account is deleted?

Per the programme terms, if an account is permanently deleted all unused points expire. Points also cannot be transferred between accounts or cashed out — they are redeemable only as DOODLY Wallet credit.

✅
Best practice — change rates deliberately, not casually

Because these settings drive real money (redemptions become wallet credit) and every automatic earn across the platform, treat a change like a policy decision: adjust one thing at a time, note the business reason, Save, and confirm the audit entry. For temporary boosts prefer a time-boxed bonus campaign over editing base rates, so earning returns to normal automatically when the end date passes.
