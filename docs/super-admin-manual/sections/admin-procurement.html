Procurement

Record every day's raw A2 buffalo-milk collection from partner farmers — quantity, quality readings, batch price and payment — with each accepted batch flowing straight into raw-milk inventory.

The Procurement screen is the daily operations desk for the Supply team. Each time a farmer's milk is collected it becomes one batch record here: how many litres came in, the fat and SNF readings taken at the collection centre, the amount payable to that farmer (calculated from their agreed rate), whether the batch was accepted, whether it has passed a quality test, and whether the farmer has been paid. The page reads live from the DOODLY database and reuses the same Procurement, Farmer and QualityTest records that power the Farmers and Quality Testing screens — so a batch you record here immediately shows up in that farmer's supply history and on the Quality queue.

ℹ️
Note — one shared record, three screens

A procurement batch is not a separate copy of data. The same batch appears under Admin → Supply → Farmers (as that farmer's collection and payment history) and under Admin → Supply → Quality Testing (as a test waiting to be recorded). Pricing always comes from the farmer's Procurement rate (₹/litre) set on the Farmers screen; there is no separate price to type here.

ℹ️
Reading the QC badge

Pending means the batch was accepted but no quality test has been recorded yet — it is counted in "Pending Quality". Rejected means the batch is not accepted (rejected on arrival). Once you record a test the badge flips to Passed or Failed.

ℹ️
Price is never typed here

The batch amount is always the farmer's agreed Procurement rate multiplied by the litres — it is calculated on the server, not entered by hand. To change what a farmer is paid per litre, update their rate on Admin → Supply → Farmers first.

⚠️
Warning — accepting adds stock immediately

Saving with Accept collection ticked increments raw-milk inventory (MILK_RAW) by the litres you entered, inside one atomic transaction. If you later reject the batch from the Manage drawer, that inventory increment is not automatically reversed — adjust raw-milk stock manually on the Inventory screen if you accept a batch in error.

⚠️
Warning — Quality Managers cannot record procurement, Procurement Managers cannot record tests

The Record test button posts to the Quality module, so it needs quality:create. A Procurement Manager can create and settle batches but only has view rights on Quality — they will get a 403 when trying to record a test. A Quality user is the opposite: they can record tests but only view procurement. Super Admin and Admin can do both.

ℹ️
Bulk operations available on the API

The procurement bulk endpoint accepts four actions — accept, reject, markPaid and markDue — over up to 2,000 batches at once (each audited). The Manage drawer surfaces markPaid for a single batch today; the other three are available for scripted or future bulk-settlement use with procurement:edit rights.

Working practices & support

Best practice, troubleshooting and common questions.

✅
Best Practice — close each day's batches

Aim to leave "Pending Quality" at zero before the end of each shift: record a test for every accepted batch, then settle payments. This keeps the Payable tile honest, gives farmers timely payment, and ensures raw-milk inventory reflects only quality-checked stock.

💡
Tip — let batch numbers auto-generate

Leave the Batch no. field blank unless you have a physical label to match. The system generates a guaranteed-unique PB-YYYYMMDD-NNNN number, which avoids "batch already exists" conflicts from hand-typed values.

Frequently asked questions

Q. Where do the ₹/litre rates come from?

A. From each farmer's Procurement rate on the Farmers screen. When you save a batch, the server sets the amount to that rate × litres — you never type a price on the Procurement screen.

Q. Does recording a collection change stock?

A. Yes, if Accept collection is ticked. The batch's litres are added to the raw-milk inventory item (MILK_RAW) in the same transaction. Rejected batches do not touch inventory.

Q. Can I see a farmer's full supply and payment history?

A. Yes — open that farmer on Admin → Supply → Farmers. Their collection, quality and payment history is built from these same procurement batches, so nothing is duplicated.

Q. What is the difference between "Pending" and "Rejected" in the QC column?

A. Pending = accepted but no quality test recorded yet. Rejected = the batch was not accepted at collection. After you record a test, the badge becomes Passed or Failed.
