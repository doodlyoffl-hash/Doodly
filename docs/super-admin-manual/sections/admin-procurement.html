Procurement

Record every day's milk collection from the farms, check its quality, and settle what each farmer is paid.

The Procurement module is DOODLY's daily milk-intake book. Each row is one collection batch — the milk one farm handed over on one day, with its quantity in litres, its fat and SNF readings, the rate paid per litre, the money owed, and its quality result. The screen shows a live KPI dashboard, a table of recent batches, and two pop-up dialogs — New procurement (record a fresh collection) and Manage (accept or reject a batch, record its quality test, and mark it paid). Everything on the page reads and writes the production database through the /api/admin/procurement* endpoints, so what you see is the true state of today's supply.

ℹ️
Note — one shared supply record, no duplicate tables

A procurement batch is not a private table for this screen. Every batch is stored on the shared Procurement record, its price comes from the linked Farmer's rate per litre, and its quality is the linked Quality Test. That is why Procurement, Farmers and Quality Testing always agree — there is one source of truth. When you accept a collection, the milk is also added to the raw-milk stock item (MILK_RAW) in Inventory. The milk type is fixed platform-wide as A2 Buffalo.

Note — the price is not typed here

There is no rate field on this form. The payable amount is worked out for you as the farm's rate per litre × the litres you enter, using the rate stored on the Farmer record. The live "Payable" line under the fields previews it as you type, and the exact figure is confirmed in the success toast (e.g. "Batch PB-20260712-4821 recorded · ₹2,790.00"). To change what a farm is paid per litre, edit the farm on the Farmers screen.

Tip — leave the batch number blank

Unless your operation numbers batches by hand, leave Batch no. empty and let the system mint a unique one in the form PB-YYYYMMDD-####. This avoids duplicate-batch conflicts and keeps codes consistent across every collection centre.

⚠️
Warning — "Mark paid" is a money action

Mark paid stamps this batch as settled. On this screen it is used one batch at a time, and there is no "un-pay" button in the dialog. Only mark a batch paid after the farmer has genuinely been paid, because that stamp is what the Payable KPI and the payment filter rely on.

ℹ️
Note — a Procurement Manager cannot record the quality test

Recording the test posts to the Quality module, which requires quality:create. The Procurement Manager role has only view on quality, so it will get a "Your role can't record quality (403)" message. Recording the pass/fail is done by a Super Admin, Admin or the Quality role — here or on the Quality Testing screen. Accepting the batch and marking it paid, however, are fully within the Procurement Manager's rights.

ℹ️
Note — bulk settlement endpoint

The platform exposes POST /api/admin/procurement/bulk for batch-wide moves — accept, reject, markPaid and markDue across up to 2,000 batches at once (all need procurement:edit). The Manage dialog uses this endpoint for a single batch when you click "Mark paid"; the same endpoint powers reporting-side bulk settlement. Every run is audited as procurement.bulk.<action>.

⛔
Caution — real stock, real money

Client-side role hiding is only for tidiness — the real security boundary is enforced on every API call. Accepting a collection changes physical raw-milk stock, and marking a batch paid changes real money owed to a real farmer. Treat every save on this screen as a production action, not a test.

Where does the payable amount come from?

From the farm, not this form. When you save a collection the server multiplies the farm's rate per litre (stored on the Farmer record) by the litres you entered. To change a farm's rate, edit it on the Farmers screen; every future batch then prices at the new rate.

What does "Accept collection" actually do?

Ticked (the default), it accepts the milk and adds the litres to the raw-milk stock item (MILK_RAW) in Inventory, in the same transaction that creates the batch. Unticked, the batch is still recorded but not added to stock, and it shows as "Rejected" until it is accepted or tested.

Can I record a quality test from the Procurement screen?

Yes, from the Manage dialog — but only while the batch has no test yet, and only if your role can create quality tests (Super Admin, Admin or Quality). A Procurement Manager, who has view-only on quality, must use the Quality Testing screen or ask a quality user.

Why is there no search box or pager on the table?

This table is configured with the toolbar and pager switched off, so it shows the newest 300 batches in one continuous view. The list endpoint still supports search and date/quality/payment filters for reporting and saved views.

Why is the milk type always "A2 Buffalo"?

DOODLY sources a single milk type, so it is fixed platform-wide as a constant rather than a per-batch field.
