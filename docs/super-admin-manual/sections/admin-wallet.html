Wallet Management

See every customer's DOODLY Wallet balance, post manual credits, debits and bottle-deposit refunds, reverse any entry, run wallet reports, and set the Trial-Pack cashback and Add-Money rules — all backed by the live database.

ℹ️
What a wallet is

Every customer has one DOODLY Wallet. Its balance is a single running total (stored in paise on the customer's User record) and every change writes an immutable WalletTxn row with the balance after the change and a unique reference. Money reaches a wallet from Trial-Pack cashback, referral rewards, promo credits, bottle-deposit refunds, customer "Add Money" recharges, and manual admin adjustments. It leaves the wallet only when a customer applies it at checkout, or an admin debits or reverses it. Because the balance is a real ledger, nothing here can be edited or deleted — you can only post a new opposite entry.

ℹ️
Live vs. cached

The KPI strip and banner are computed fresh in the database on every page load. The Wallets and Reports tabs render from a local mirror of the same data (the page fetches the wallet list, up to 2,000 transactions, and the cashback config, then caches them) so the tabs are instant. Every money action you take is sent to the backend first, and the whole page reloads from the database the moment it succeeds — so what you see always matches the ledger.

⛔
Caution — money moves the moment you confirm

Credit, Debit and Refund deposit all post a real ledger entry against a live customer wallet the instant you finish the prompts. There is no "undo" button — the only way to cancel a mistake is to open the wallet's history and Reverse the entry, which posts a matching opposite entry (both stay visible forever). Read the amount back before confirming.

⚠️
Reversal never deletes

Reversing does not remove the original entry. Both the original and the new "Reversal of WTX-…" entry stay in the ledger for good, and the balance nets out. This is by design — the wallet is an audit-grade ledger, so every correction is itself recorded.

✅
Best Practice — the cashback is one-time by design

You never need to guard against paying a customer twice. The Trial-Pack cashback is protected in the database by a unique key on the customer id (TrialCashback.userId), so a customer can only ever be credited once — even under simultaneous payments. The panel's own note says so: "Benefit is one-time per customer (enforced by the backend)."

ℹ️
How a customer recharge actually credits the wallet

When a customer taps Add Money they pick an amount, then pay on Razorpay's secure screen. Only after the payment is signature-verified does the backend post the top-up — and it is idempotent on the gateway payment id, so a retried or duplicated callback never double-credits. Frozen wallets and disabled recharge are refused up front.

✅
Best Practice — always write a real reason

Debit already forces a reason; do the same for Credit and Refund. The reason is stored on the ledger row and is the only explanation an auditor (or your future self) will have. Prefer specifics — "Goodwill: spoilt delivery order #DZ284913" beats "Manual credit".

Frequently asked questions

Can I edit or delete a wrong transaction?

No. The wallet is an append-only ledger. To correct a mistake, open the wallet's history and Reverse the entry — a matching opposite entry is posted and both remain visible.

If I reverse a Trial-Pack cashback, can the customer earn it again?

Yes. Reversing a cashback voids the one-time cashback record, so the customer becomes eligible again the next time they pay for an eligible plan.

Why is a customer missing from the Wallets list?

The list only includes customers who have a balance above zero or at least one wallet transaction. A brand-new customer who has never earned, spent or been credited won't appear until their first wallet activity.

Does turning off "Trial cashback enabled" claw back credits already given?

No. It only stops future cashbacks from being issued. Balances already credited stay with customers until spent; to remove one you must Debit or Reverse it deliberately.

What's the difference between the KPI strip and the Reports tab?

The KPI strip is computed live in the database on every load (whole-programme totals and today's activity). The Reports tab lets you pick a date range and focuses on cashback issuance, usage, outstanding balance and Trial-Pack conversion.
